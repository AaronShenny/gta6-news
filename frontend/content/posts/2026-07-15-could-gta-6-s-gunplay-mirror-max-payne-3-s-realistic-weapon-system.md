# Could GTA 6's Gunplay Mirror Max Payne 3's Realistic Weapon System?

A recent discussion on Reddit, initiated by user /u/Swimming-Way-2696, explores the possibility of Grand Theft Auto 6's gunplay taking inspiration from Max Payne 3 and Red Dead Redemption 2. The user highlights similarities between Max Payne 3 and RDR2's combat mechanics, such as the ability to carry a main firearm in the left hand and a sidearm in the right, alongside a shared emphasis on combat realism and weapon detail. A key distinction noted in Max Payne 3 is the mechanic of tossing a pistol when out of ammo and switching back to the main weapon. The article posits that GTA V's extensive inventory, allowing players to carry over 50 weapons, made such a detailed and realistic system impractical. For GTA 6, the speculation suggests a potential shift towards a more limited weapon loadout, possibly restricting players to two sidearms and one or two main weapons, akin to the systems in RDR2 and Max Payne 3, thereby enhancing realism and tactical depth.

## Key Takeaways
- GTA 6's gunplay might draw inspiration from Max Payne 3 and Red Dead Redemption 2.
- Max Payne 3 and RDR2 share similarities like dual-wielding (main firearm left, sidearm right) and detailed combat realism.
- Max Payne 3 features a unique mechanic where players toss empty pistols and switch to their main weapon.
- GTA V's large weapon inventory (50+ weapons) was cited as a reason for not implementing such realistic mechanics.
- The speculation suggests GTA 6 could limit weapon slots (e.g., two sidearms, one or two main weapons) to enhance realism.
