# Should Rockstar abandon tap X to run in GTA 6?

A recent discussion thread on the /r/GTA6 subreddit, initiated by user /u/Pistoluislero, has reignited the debate surrounding Rockstar's long-standing 'tap X to run' control scheme. While the original poster personally finds the system perfectly acceptable and has never been bothered by it in past titles, they acknowledge that a significant portion of the gaming community perceives it as an outdated mechanic. The core of the discussion revolves around whether Rockstar should overhaul this control method for the highly anticipated Grand Theft Auto 6, if they are likely to do so, and what the collective community preference is regarding character movement controls in the next installment.

## Key Takeaways
- The 'tap X to run' mechanic is a common control scheme in Rockstar games.
- Some players, including the original poster, do not find the mechanic annoying or outdated.
- A notable segment of the gaming community views 'tap X to run' as an outdated system.
- The discussion questions if Rockstar should, or will, change this mechanic for GTA 6.
- Community input is sought regarding preferred character movement controls.

## FAQ
**What is the 'tap X to run' mechanic being discussed for GTA 6?**
It refers to a control scheme, common in Rockstar games, where players repeatedly tap a specific button (like 'X' on PlayStation controllers) to make their character sprint.

**Why is this mechanic a point of debate for GTA 6?**
While some players find it unobtrusive, many in the gaming community consider it outdated or cumbersome, leading to discussions on whether Rockstar should modernize character movement controls for the upcoming game.

**Does everyone dislike the 'tap X to run' system?**
No, the user who initiated the discussion on Reddit stated they personally never found it annoying. However, they acknowledge that many other players do.
