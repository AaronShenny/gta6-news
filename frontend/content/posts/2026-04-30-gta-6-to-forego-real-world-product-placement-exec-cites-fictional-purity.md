# GTA 6 to Forego Real-World Product Placement, Exec Cites Fictional Purity

Take-Two Interactive CEO Strauss Zelnick has confirmed that Grand Theft Auto 6 will not incorporate real-world product placement or brand integration deals. Speaking at iicon in Las Vegas, Zelnick stated this decision is vital to maintain the integrity of the game's fictional world and remain 'true to the underlying intellectual property' and consumers, asserting that 'all the brands are made up' to keep the experience 'pure.' This approach aligns with the GTA series' long-standing tradition of parodying real-world companies, such as 'Lifeinvader' (Facebook) and 'Cluckin' Bell' (KFC), with fans anticipating a Disney parody in the Florida-set GTA 6. Despite the game's unprecedented hype, Zelnick also admitted to being 'scared' about its release, emphasizing the importance of staying humble in the entertainment business. GTA 6 faces immense pressure to succeed, following its predecessor GTA 5's sales of over 225 million copies. The highly anticipated title is set to launch on November 19 for PS5 and Xbox Series X|S, with Zelnick previously indicating a 'reasonable' price point.
