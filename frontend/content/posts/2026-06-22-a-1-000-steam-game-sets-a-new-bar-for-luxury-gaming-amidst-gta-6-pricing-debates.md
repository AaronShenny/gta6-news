# A $1,000 Steam Game Sets a New Bar for 'Luxury Gaming' Amidst GTA 6 Pricing Debates

Amidst growing speculation that GTA 6 preorders might usher in a new era of $80 standard edition games for PS5 and Xbox Series X|S, a new Steam release, 'Congratulations On Your Purchase,' has already set an extreme precedent with its staggering $1,000 price tag. Described as a 'first-person luxury experience,' the game offers players a palace setting complete with a red carpet, chandelier, and a velvet rope barrier. Notably, it boasts no combat, enemies, quests, or skill trees, instead providing 'the feeling of having arrived somewhere important' via a single in-game box.

Developer Minimal Viable Prestige is remarkably candid about the game's value proposition, directly addressing players with: 'You paid for this. Not accidentally. Not on impulse. You saw the price. You read the description. And then you bought it anyway. Welcome.' Despite its high cost and sparse content, Steam's standard refund policy applies, allowing returns within two hours of playtime or two weeks of ownership.

While 'Congratulations On Your Purchase' stands out for its minimal offering at such a high price, the article notes that other special editions have historically commanded significant sums, often providing substantial physical extras. Examples include a $750 Resident Evil 6 'Stylish Edition' with a replica leather jacket, an $800 Assassin's Creed Origins collector's edition, a $190,000 Grid 2 edition that came with an actual car, a Dying Light player's custom zombie apocalypse shelter, and a $1 million Saints Row 4 special edition promising a trip to space. The existence of 'Congratulations On Your Purchase' highlights the wild spectrum of pricing in the gaming industry, from potential $80 blockbusters like GTA 6 to this unique, high-priced digital statement.
