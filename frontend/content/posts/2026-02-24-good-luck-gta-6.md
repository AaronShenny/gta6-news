# Good luck GTA 6

A long-time player of Grand Theft Auto Online has voiced serious apprehension regarding the future of GTA 6, particularly its online component. The Reddit post, titled 'Good luck GTA 6,' highlights the pervasive issue of hackers in GTA Online lobbies, a problem the user claims has persisted for 13 years across various platforms, including the Xbox 360 era. The author warns that players are no longer as tolerant or unaware of malicious users as they were in 2013. If Rockstar Games fails to drastically enhance its anti-cheat measures for GTA 6, the user predicts its online lifespan will be significantly shorter than GTA 5's, potentially dying within a year due to an unplayable environment.

## Key Takeaways
- GTA Online lobbies are currently overrun with malicious hackers.
- The anti-cheat problem has persisted for approximately 13 years, dating back to GTA 5's launch on Xbox 360.
- Players are more aware and less tolerant of hackers now compared to 2013.
- If Rockstar Games does not significantly improve its anti-cheat for GTA 6, its online component is predicted to fail rapidly, potentially within a year.
