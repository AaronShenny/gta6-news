# GTA VI Hype Endures: Journalist Dispels Ultimate Edition Fears, Defends Rockstar's Launch Strategy

Despite a wave of negative press and widespread criticisms, a dedicated gaming journalist maintains strong anticipation for Grand Theft Auto VI, declaring it the potential 'game of the decade.' The author systematically debunks popular complaints, arguing that claims of essential story content or shops being locked behind the Ultimate Edition are unfounded, drawing parallels to previous Rockstar titles. They also defend the staggered launch of Online mode, citing Rockstar's historical release patterns and noting a new standalone purchase option for GTA Online. While acknowledging the disappointment of physical preorders being digital codes and the slow marketing pace, the journalist expresses unwavering confidence in Rockstar and has already secured their Ultimate Edition, urging fellow fans not to succumb to 'asinine negativity.'

## FAQ
**Is essential story content or shops locked behind GTA VI's Ultimate Edition?**
According to the journalist, claims of essential story content or shops being locked behind the Ultimate Edition are unsubstantiated. They argue that previous Rockstar titles only offered additional, non-integral content in their deluxe editions, and GTA VI is expected to follow this pattern.

**Will GTA VI Online be available at launch?**
No, the journalist confirms that GTA Online will not be available at launch. This aligns with Rockstar's past release strategies for games like GTA V and RDR2, where Online mode was introduced weeks or months after the main story's release. A new, cheaper standalone purchase option for Online will also be available.

**What are the legitimate concerns acknowledged by the journalist?**
The journalist acknowledges legitimate disappointment regarding the 'physical' preorder being just a digital code. They also express frustration with Rockstar's 'slow burn' marketing strategy, fearing it might cause the overall hype to become stale.

**Why is the journalist still hyped for GTA VI despite the bad press?**
The journalist believes GTA VI will be the 'game of the decade' and trusts Rockstar's track record, pointing out that similar public doubting cycles occurred with previous titles. They refuse to let 'asinine negativity' ruin their anticipated enjoyment after a 13-year wait.
