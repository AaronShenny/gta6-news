# GTA 6's Special Edition Content: Is the Outrage Selective, Given Rockstar's History?

Recent discussions regarding Grand Theft Auto VI have highlighted a growing discontent among players concerning content locked behind more expensive special editions. However, this article from a Reddit user, framed as a journalist's perspective, challenges the perceived 'selective outrage,' arguing that Rockstar Games has consistently employed this monetization strategy for years. The author points to a history of similar practices dating back to Red Dead Redemption 1, Red Dead Redemption 2, and Grand Theft Auto V. While acknowledging that locking content is a valid point of criticism, the piece questions why this issue has become a significant talking point only with GTA 6, pondering if it's due to the game's unprecedented scrutiny or unique aspects of its release.

## Key Takeaways
- There is a growing backlash against Rockstar Games for locking content behind more expensive special editions of GTA 6.
- The author argues that this practice is not new for Rockstar, citing Red Dead Redemption 1, RDR2, and GTA V as previous examples.
- The article questions the 'selective outrage,' suggesting that if the practice was wrong before, it remains wrong now, and criticism should be consistent.
- It raises the question of whether the heightened criticism for GTA 6 is due to its massive anticipation and scrutiny or specific elements unique to this release.
