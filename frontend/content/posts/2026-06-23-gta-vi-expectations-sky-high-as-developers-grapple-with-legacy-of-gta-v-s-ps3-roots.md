# GTA VI Expectations Sky-High as Developers Grapple with Legacy of GTA V's PS3 Roots

As anticipation builds for GTA VI, a recent discussion emphasizes the colossal expectations facing Rockstar Games, largely due to the groundbreaking success of GTA V. The original GTA V, launched in 2013, was developed for the PlayStation 3, a console with significantly limited hardware compared to today's machines. Despite running on a GPU 54 times weaker and with 34 times less RAM than a PS5, GTA V delivered a visually stunning experience for its era. This legacy has set an incredibly high bar, positioning player expectations as Rockstar's primary competition for GTA VI, which is being designed for hardware roughly 50 times more powerful than the PS3.

## Key Takeaways
- GTA V was originally developed and released in 2013 for the PlayStation 3.
- The PS3's hardware was remarkably constrained, featuring a GPU 54 times weaker and 256MB of RAM (34 times less) compared to a PlayStation 5.
- Despite these limitations, GTA V achieved phenomenal visual quality for its time.
- Rockstar Games faces the immense challenge of managing player expectations for GTA VI, which are elevated by the vast technological leap in current-gen consoles (approximately 50 times more powerful than the PS3).
- The article argues that player expectations, rather than external competitors, are Rockstar's biggest hurdle for GTA VI.

## FAQ
**What console was GTA V originally designed for?**
GTA V was originally designed for the PlayStation 3, released in 2013.

**How much more powerful are current-gen consoles compared to the PS3?**
Current-gen consoles like the PS5 are roughly 50 times more powerful than the PS3, with GPUs being around 54 times stronger and RAM being 34 times greater.

**What is Rockstar's biggest challenge with GTA VI?**
Rockstar's biggest challenge with GTA VI is managing the extremely high player expectations, which have been set by GTA V's impressive technical achievements on much weaker hardware and the significant advancements in current-gen console capabilities.
