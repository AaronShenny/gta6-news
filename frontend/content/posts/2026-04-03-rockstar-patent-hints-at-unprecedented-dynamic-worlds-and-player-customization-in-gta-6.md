# Rockstar Patent Hints at Unprecedented Dynamic Worlds and Player Customization in GTA 6

A patent filed by Rockstar Games, titled "System and method for game object and environment generation" (US10987587B2), outlines a sophisticated method for automatically creating game environments and interiors based on keyword parameters. This technology allows for dynamic adjustments, such as changing a neighborhood's aesthetic from "worn_down" to "renovated" based on player actions, by replacing assets tagged accordingly. Crucially, the patent also suggests an in-game interface for players to directly edit these generated objects and furniture, implying deep customization options for player-owned spaces. For Grand Theft Auto VI, this system could mean a massive number of unique and evolving interiors, reducing repetition and offering a constantly fresh experience, akin to Red Dead Redemption II's diverse pedestrian appearances. This makes the feasibility of a high volume of interiors, like 70% of 700, much more reasonable if many are procedurally generated.

## Key Takeaways
- Rockstar patent US10987587B2 details a system for automatic game environment and object generation.
- Environments are created using keyword parameters (e.g., 'old', 'worn_down', 'new', 'hipster') and adjustable values (e.g., dirtiness, number of items).
- The system enables dynamic world changes based on in-game events or player progression, such as neighborhoods improving over time.
- An in-game interface is mentioned, suggesting players can customize objects and furniture within their own properties.
- For GTA 6, this implies a vast number of unique, procedurally generated interiors, enhancing replayability and reducing environmental repetition.
- The technology makes the creation of a large volume of interiors more feasible by combining unique and dynamically generated content.

## FAQ
**What is the Rockstar patent US10987587B2 about?**
This patent details a system for procedurally generating game environments, objects, and interiors using keyword-based parameters to define their characteristics and appearance.

**How does this technology impact GTA 6's world?**
It suggests GTA 6 could feature highly dynamic environments that evolve based on player actions, and a much larger number of unique and varied interiors, offering a less repetitive and more immersive experience.

**Will players be able to customize their in-game spaces?**
Yes, the patent mentions an in-game interface allowing players to edit parameters, implying direct customization of furniture and objects within their properties.

**Does this mean all environments in GTA 6 will be procedurally generated?**
Not necessarily all. The patent suggests a mix of unique and procedurally generated content, making a high volume of diverse interiors more achievable without requiring every single one to be hand-crafted.
