# Community Analysis Suggests GTA 6's Vice City Map Is Colossally Larger Than Previous Rockstar Worlds

A recent community effort, detailed by Reddit user /u/PirloDiLatte, has attempted to quantify the immense scale of Grand Theft Auto 6's upcoming Vice City map. By overlaying the maps of previous Rockstar titles onto the projected Vice City layout, the analysis astonishingly suggests that GTA 6's main metropolis could comfortably house all three cities from GTA San Andreas, the complete map of GTA 4, and even the dense urban environment of Saint Denis from Red Dead Redemption 2 – all while still leaving significant unused space. The experiment, while acknowledging potential inaccuracies due to the challenging format of Vice City's layout and some water bodies, highlights the sheer vastness. It also notes the surprising relative smallness of Saint Denis, attributing its perceived grandeur in RDR2 to its high density and player traversal methods rather than sheer area. Furthermore, the report indicates that experienced mappers believe the final map could be even larger than currently estimated, reinforcing the expectation that Vice City in GTA 6 will offer an unparalleled sense of scale.

## Key Takeaways
- GTA 6's Vice City map is projected to be exceptionally large.
- Community analysis suggests it can fit all three GTA San Andreas cities, the entire GTA 4 map, and Saint Denis.
- Significant space is expected to remain even after fitting these previous game areas.
- Red Dead Redemption 2's Saint Denis appears relatively small in comparison, its perceived size in RDR2 attributed to density and lack of vehicle travel.
- Mappers indicate the actual final map size could be even larger than current estimations.
