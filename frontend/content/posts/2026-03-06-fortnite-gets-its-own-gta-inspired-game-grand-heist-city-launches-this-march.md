# Fortnite Gets Its Own GTA-Inspired Game: Grand Heist City Launches This March

Jogo Games, led by popular content creator Andre "Typical Gamer" Rebelo, is set to launch "Grand Heist City," a new open-world game within Fortnite that openly draws inspiration from the highly anticipated Grand Theft Auto series, particularly GTA 6. Releasing on March 14, 2026, the game, developed using the Unreal Editor for Fortnite (UEFN) and backed by an Epic Mega Grant, promises the "chaotic energy" of GTA with an explorable open map, missions, heists, and multiplayer. Rebelo, a lifelong GTA fan, aims for Grand Heist City to serve as a free interim experience for players eagerly awaiting GTA 6, which itself is scheduled for a November 19, 2026 release. While previous Jogo projects were microtransaction-free, Grand Heist City will incorporate them, though specific details remain unconfirmed.

## Key Takeaways
- Jogo Games, led by Andre "Typical Gamer" Rebelo, is launching "Grand Heist City" within Fortnite.
- The game is heavily inspired by the Grand Theft Auto series, especially GTA 6, even mimicking its key art.
- "Grand Heist City" is set to launch on March 14, 2026.
- Developed using the Unreal Editor for Fortnite (UEFN) and partially funded by an Epic Mega Grant.
- Features an open map, missions, heists, multiplayer, and aims to capture GTA's "chaotic energy."
- Designed as a free alternative or "meantime" experience for players awaiting GTA 6.
- Will include microtransactions, a first for Jogo's Fortnite projects, though details are undisclosed.
- GTA 6 is scheduled for a November 19, 2026 release on PS5 and Xbox Series X|S.
