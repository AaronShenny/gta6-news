# Opinion: A Gaming Journalist's Hopes for GTA 6's Narrative Direction

A passionate gaming journalist, reflecting on a YouTube comment, argues that GTA 6 should be vastly different from GTA V, particularly in its storytelling. Contrary to the YouTube commenter's negative take on a potential divergence, the journalist expresses hope that GTA 6 avoids GTA V's narrative style, which they deem the 'worst story' in the franchise. The core criticism of GTA V centers on its '90 percent slop satire' lacking intelligence, dated dialogue, and unengaging characters, even finding Franklin's story, which they enjoyed most, to be rushed and poorly written. The journalist contrasts this with earlier GTA titles (GTA 3, GTA 4, San Andreas, Vice City) and Red Dead Redemption 2, which blended serious stories with humor and smart satire, advocating for a more serious, character-driven narrative for GTA 6.

## Key Takeaways
- The author hopes GTA 6 will be vastly different from GTA V, specifically regarding its story.
- GTA V is criticized for having the 'worst story' in the Grand Theft Auto franchise.
- Key criticisms of GTA V include its '90 percent slop satire' that is not 'smart,' dated dialogue, and poorly written, unengaging characters.
- The author cites earlier GTA titles (GTA 3, GTA 4, San Andreas, Vice City) and Red Dead Redemption 2 as examples of balancing serious narratives with humor and satire.
- A desire for GTA 6 to feature a more serious story with genuine characters is expressed.
