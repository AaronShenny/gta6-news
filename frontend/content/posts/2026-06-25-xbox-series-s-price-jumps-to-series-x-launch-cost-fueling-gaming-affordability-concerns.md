# Xbox Series S Price Jumps to Series X Launch Cost, Fueling Gaming Affordability Concerns

In a stunning reversal of historical console pricing trends, the Xbox Series S, originally positioned as Microsoft's affordable entry into next-gen gaming at $300, is set to increase its price to $500 starting August 1st. This new price point remarkably matches the launch cost of the more powerful Xbox Series X from November 2020, representing a staggering 67% hike for the less powerful console. The article attributes this unprecedented surge to an AI-driven chip shortage, a situation Microsoft itself has contributed to as a major player in AI products. This dramatic shift raises significant concerns about gaming accessibility, effectively pricing out budget-conscious consumers who might have previously relied on the Series S to play major upcoming titles like GTA 6.

## Key Takeaways
- The Xbox Series S price is increasing to $500 starting August 1st.
- This new price matches the Xbox Series X's launch price from November 2020.
- The price increase represents a 67% hike for the Series S since its launch.
- The primary reason for the price surge is an AI-driven chip shortage, impacting components like RAM and storage.
- Microsoft, a significant player in AI products, has contributed to the demand fueling the chip shortage.
- This move contradicts historical console pricing, where hardware typically becomes cheaper over time.
- The increased cost exacerbates the growing affordability crisis in gaming, making titles like GTA 6 less accessible for price-conscious consumers.
- Some retailers, like Amazon, may temporarily still offer the Series S for around $380, though this is still higher than its original launch price.
