# GTA 6 NPCs Poised for Unprecedented Animation Depth and Reactivity

A deep dive into a 2020 Rockstar Games patent, 'System and method for virtual character locomotion,' reveals ambitious plans for Grand Theft Auto 6's non-player characters. The patent outlines a sophisticated animation system built on four distinct levels: Archetype (e.g., male, female, police), Basic movement (e.g., walking, running through different terrains), Character state (e.g., injured, drunk), and Equipment (e.g., carrying items, armed). This multi-layered approach is expected to deliver significantly smoother, more natural animation transitions compared to GTA V, eliminating jarring shifts. Furthermore, the system is anticipated to enable unprecedented NPC reactivity to player actions, promising a much larger scale of realism and immersion than even Red Dead Redemption 2's improvements.

## Key Takeaways
- A 2020 Rockstar Games patent, 'System and method for virtual character locomotion,' details advanced NPC animation technology.
- NPC movement will be influenced by four layered levels: Archetype (e.g., male, female, police), Basic movement (e.g., walking, running through water/sand), Character state (e.g., injured, drunk, tired), and Equipment (e.g., carrying items, armed).
- A major improvement over GTA V will be significantly smoother, more natural animation transitions, eliminating jarring shifts.
- The system is expected to facilitate highly reactive NPC behavior to player actions, such as a single gunshot.
- The scale of these animation improvements is anticipated to be much greater than those seen in Red Dead Redemption 2.
