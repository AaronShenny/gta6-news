# GTA 6: Fan Pitches Revolutionary Narrative Shift, Advocating for Love, Heartbreak, and Non-Criminal Endings

A passionate Reddit user, u/onetimepost07, has sparked discussion within the Grand Theft Auto community by proposing a significant narrative overhaul for the highly anticipated GTA 6. The article, originally a Reddit post, argues that Rockstar Games should move beyond its conventional "criminal bad ending" tropes, which often involve character death or betrayal. Instead, the author advocates for a storyline that prioritizes themes of love, heartbreak, and healing, offering protagonists a genuine chance at a life beyond crime. The core of the suggestion centers on GTA 6's rumored protagonists, Jason and Lucia. The user envisions a pivotal choice for Jason: to either remain entangled with Lucia and a life of crime or to seek a real, non-chaotic future for himself. Lucia's ultimate decision to join him or continue her criminal path would be dynamically influenced by the player's choices and character development throughout the game, leading to multiple, emotionally resonant endings. Critiquing the perceived repetitiveness of narrative conclusions in past Rockstar titles like Red Dead Redemption 2 and Grand Theft Auto V, the post calls for "unexpected" outcomes, drawing parallels to the more hopeful fates of RDR2 characters like Charles who moved to Canada, or Sadie Adler who transitioned to a legitimate life. The overarching plea is for a "gut-wrenching" story that challenges players with genuinely difficult moral dilemmas, forcing a choice between an "easy way out" and a "genuinely necessary and painful ending," thereby pushing narrative boundaries beyond the traditional criminal saga.

## Key Takeaways
- Rockstar should abandon typical criminal endings (death, betrayal) in GTA 6.
- The narrative should focus on themes of love, heartbreak, and healing.
- Protagonist Jason could choose between Lucia/crime or a life away from chaos.
- Lucia's destiny should be influenced by player choices, leading to multiple character-driven endings.
- The endings in past Rockstar games (RDR2, GTA 5) are perceived as repetitive.
- The author desires more "unexpected" and hopeful endings, similar to Charles or Sadie in RDR2.
- GTA 6's story should present difficult moral choices, contrasting an "easy way out" with a "painful, necessary ending."
