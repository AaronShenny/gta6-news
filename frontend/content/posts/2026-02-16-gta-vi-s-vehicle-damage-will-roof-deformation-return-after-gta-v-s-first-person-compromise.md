# GTA VI's Vehicle Damage: Will Roof Deformation Return After GTA V's First-Person Compromise?

A recent discussion sparked on Reddit's r/GTA6 subreddit by user /u/mahirbr highlights a curious detail from Grand Theft Auto V: the removal of detailed roof damage on vehicles following the introduction of the first-person camera mode. This change was likely implemented to prevent visual glitches and clipping issues when players viewed the car's interior from a first-person perspective. The community is now pondering how Rockstar Games will approach this challenge in the highly anticipated Grand Theft Auto VI, especially given the expected advancements in graphical fidelity and immersion. Players are eager to see if GTA VI will reintroduce realistic roof deformation while maintaining a seamless first-person experience, or if a different solution will be found.

## Key Takeaways
- GTA V removed detailed roof damage after the introduction of the first-person camera.
- The change was likely made to prevent visual clipping and other issues in first-person view.
- The gaming community is speculating on how GTA VI will handle vehicle damage, particularly roof deformation.
- Players hope for a return to realistic damage while maintaining a seamless first-person experience in GTA VI.

## FAQ
**Why was roof damage removed in GTA V?**
Detailed roof damage was likely removed in Grand Theft Auto V to avoid visual clipping and glitches when players used the first-person camera mode, which offered an interior view of vehicles.

**Will GTA VI feature realistic vehicle damage?**
While Rockstar Games has not officially detailed vehicle damage mechanics for GTA VI, the community is hopeful for a return to or even an improvement upon realistic deformation, including roof damage, possibly with new technical solutions to overcome past challenges.
