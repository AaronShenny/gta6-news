# GTA 6's Random Events Could Redefine Open-World Immersion, Says Fan

The article, originally a Reddit post by u/Zealousideal_Roll827, expresses immense anticipation for Grand Theft Auto 6, particularly highlighting the potential of its random events and dynamic world interactions. Drawing heavily from experiences in Red Dead Redemption 2, the author recounts a single in-game journey where they encountered multiple spontaneous events: a Lemoyne Raider ambush, NPCs cracking a safe, a hostile camper, and a shooting challenge. These varied encounters, along with smaller moments like giving a drunk a ride or discovering a Klan meeting, illustrate RDR2's ability to make the game world feel "lived in" and offer significant replay value. The author extrapolates this concept to the modern setting of GTA 6's Leonida, imagining scenarios like being robbed by a gang in La Perle or deciding whether to give a drunk NPC a ride home, with potential consequences. The core hope is that Rockstar will not only maintain but significantly expand upon this aspect, taking it "10x further" than RDR2 to create an even more spontaneous and immersive single-player experience.

## Key Takeaways
- Random events in RDR2 significantly enhance immersion and replay value.
