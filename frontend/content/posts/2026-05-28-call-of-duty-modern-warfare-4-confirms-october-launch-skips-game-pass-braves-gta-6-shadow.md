# Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 4 Confirms October Launch, Skips Game Pass, Braves GTA 6 Shadow

Activision has officially pulled back the curtain on Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 4, revealing an October 23 launch date across PC, PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X|S, and the upcoming Switch 2. This places the highly anticipated shooter a mere 27 days ahead of the juggernaut Grand Theft Auto 6, which is currently the only major title slated for November 19. This bold move sees Infinity Ward's latest creation, featuring a campaign centered on rookie Private Park in the Korean peninsula, defy the industry trend of avoiding GTA 6's release window.
Perhaps equally significant, MW4 will not be a day-one addition to Xbox Game Pass. Following Microsoft's new "windowing" strategy, new Call of Duty titles will arrive on the subscription service approximately a year after launch. Adding to its notable platform presence, Modern Warfare 4 marks the franchise's return to a Nintendo console since 2013, launching on the Switch 2. The game will naturally include its signature multiplayer and the popular DMZ mode.

## Key Takeaways
- Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 4 is set to release on Friday, October 23, across PC, PS5, Xbox Series X|S, and Switch 2.
- Its launch date is 27 days before Grand Theft Auto 6, which releases on November 19.
- MW4 will not be available on Xbox Game Pass at launch, aligning with Microsoft's new "windowing" strategy for new Call of Duty titles.
- The game marks the Call of Duty series' return to a Nintendo platform, launching on the Switch 2, the first since 2013.
- Infinity Ward is leading development, with the campaign focusing on rookie soldier Private Park and a story set in the Korean peninsula.
- GTA 6 remains the only major new release scheduled for November 19, as other publishers generally avoid its highly anticipated launch window.
