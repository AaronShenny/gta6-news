# GTA 6: Vice City's Population Density to Mirror Trailer's Bustle in Key Areas

A recent discussion on Reddit's r/GTA6 subreddit posits that *Grand Theft Auto 6* will feature a dynamic population density, with certain high-profile areas matching the bustling scenes depicted in the game's trailers. According to the user submission, locations such as downtown Ocean Drive and the beach are anticipated to be as densely populated as shown, while other parts of Vice City will exhibit a more moderate crowd level. This varied approach is expected to significantly enhance the realism and vibrancy of the city, creating a more authentic sense of scale and space within the game world. The post also draws a parallel to *Red Dead Redemption 2*, highlighting how its city of Saint Denis achieved remarkable density, particularly in marketplaces, even on the PlayStation 4's hardware, suggesting a precedent for Rockstar's ability to deliver crowded environments.

## Key Takeaways
- Specific areas like downtown Ocean Drive and the beach in GTA 6 are expected to match the high population density seen in the game's trailers.
- Other parts of Vice City will likely feature less dense crowds, contributing to a varied and realistic urban environment.
- This dynamic density is believed to make Vice City feel more alive, enhance realism, and improve the overall sense of space.
- The article references *Red Dead Redemption 2*'s Saint Denis as an example of Rockstar's capability to render highly dense areas on previous-gen hardware.
