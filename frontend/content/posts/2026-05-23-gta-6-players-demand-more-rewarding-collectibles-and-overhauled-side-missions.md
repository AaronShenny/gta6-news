# GTA 6: Players Demand More Rewarding Collectibles and Overhauled Side Missions

In a recent Reddit post, a dedicated Grand Theft Auto fan voiced strong opinions regarding Rockstar Games' approach to 100% completion and side mission rewards, particularly in GTA 5 and Red Dead Redemption 2. The user criticized the lack of meaningful incentives for completing tedious tasks, citing examples like GTA 5's submarine pieces yielding a mere $10 or letter scraps offering no tangible player reward. They argue that collectibles often lack real challenge, becoming simple 'YouTube guides' rather than engaging content. The post advocates for unique, gameplay-altering rewards, such as character ability boosts or special gear, and suggests transforming collectibles into more interactive experiences like detective puzzles, rather than simple fetch quests, hoping GTA 6 will deliver a more satisfying and less repetitive experience.

## Key Takeaways
- Criticism of Rockstar's current reward system for 100% completion and side missions in games like GTA 5 and RDR2.
- Examples include GTA 5's submarine pieces yielding minimal cash and letter scraps offering no tangible player reward.
- Collectibles are perceived as boring and lacking challenge, often requiring external guides.
- Suggestion for unique, gameplay-altering rewards (e.g., character speed boosts, special gear).
- Call for more engaging collectible mechanics, such as detective work and puzzles, instead of simple fetch quests.
- Hope for fewer, but more meaningful, collectibles in GTA 6.

## FAQ

Examples include collecting all submarine pieces in GTA 5 for only $10 and letter scraps yielding just a cutscene with no tangible player benefit.

**What kind of improvements are suggested for GTA 6's reward system?**
The post suggests unique, gameplay-altering rewards like character ability boosts (e.g., faster movement) or special gear (e.g., a super-fast swimming suit).

Instead of simple collection, the player suggests making collectibles more challenging and interactive, such as solving puzzles or engaging in detective work.
