# GTA 6's Scale Hailed as 'Genuinely Insane,' Dwarfing Even RDR2's Immersive World

A recent Reddit post highlights the anticipated, 'genuinely insane' scale and detail of Grand Theft Auto 6, drawing comparisons to Rockstar's previous title, Red Dead Redemption 2. The author praises RDR2's living, breathing world, attributing its success to meticulous sound design, NPC activity, diverse architecture, and dynamic weather cycles that encourage slow exploration. They argue that GTA 5, while a marvel, lacked this atmospheric variation. For GTA 6, the expectation is a massive leap, promising 'high quality CGI movie' visuals, significantly more interiors, verticality, vehicle variety, and an even greater density of detail and activity. Speculation suggests Vice City alone could rival the entire GTA 5 map in size, complemented by additional, yet-unrevealed locations, signaling an unparalleled open-world experience.

## Key Takeaways
- Red Dead Redemption 2's world is lauded for its immersive quality, driven by sound design, NPC activity, unique architecture, and intricate details.
- RDR2 encourages slow, atmospheric exploration, a contrast to GTA 5's faster-paced traversal.
- The dynamic day/night cycle and varied weather effects in RDR2 significantly enhance map replayability and atmosphere.
- GTA 6 is anticipated to achieve 'high quality CGI movie' visuals, setting a new benchmark for realism.
- The upcoming title is expected to feature increased interiors, verticality, vehicle range, detail density, NPC activity, and advanced weather variations.
- Vice City in GTA 6 is speculated to be as large as the entire GTA 5 map, with additional undisclosed locations contributing to an immense overall scale.
