# Concerns Emerge Over GTA 6 Performance on Current-Gen Consoles

A gaming journalist has voiced concerns regarding the potential performance of Grand Theft Auto VI on current-generation consoles. Drawing parallels from their experience with performance drops in demanding titles like Baldur's Gate 3, the journalist theorizes that GTA 6's ambitious scale, ultra-realistic open world, and high level of detail showcased in trailers could overwhelm base consoles like the PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series S. While acknowledging the games are different, the core argument is that if existing complex games already push hardware limits, GTA 6's density and simulation could face even greater challenges. The journalist suggests that optimal performance might only be achieved on hardware upgrades like a PS5 Pro or Xbox Series X, implying the game could incentivize players to invest in newer systems.

## Key Takeaways
- Journalist predicts GTA 6 might struggle significantly on current-gen consoles (PS5, Xbox Series S).
- Performance is expected to be 'okay' at best on more powerful systems like PS5 Pro or Xbox Series X, but not perfect.
- The theory is based on experience with performance drops in other demanding games, specifically Baldur's Gate 3's final act.
- GTA 6's 'massively ambitious, ultra-realistic open-world game' with high detail and activity is cited as the primary reason for potential issues.
- The author believes the game's demands could push players towards upgrading their hardware.
- This assessment is presented as a personal theory based on individual gaming experience.
