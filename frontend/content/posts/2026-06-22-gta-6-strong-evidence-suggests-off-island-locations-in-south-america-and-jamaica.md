# GTA 6: Strong Evidence Suggests Off-Island Locations in South America and Jamaica

A dedicated fan journalist presents a compelling case for the inclusion of off-island locations and gameplay in Grand Theft Auto 6, specifically pointing towards South America (Colombia) and possibly Jamaica. The argument is built on several key observations: Firstly, a consistent trend in recent Rockstar titles (Red Dead Redemption 1 & 2, GTA V) to feature significant gameplay in areas away from the main map, with Guarma in RDR2 potentially serving as a testing ground for complex jungle and beach environments. Secondly, an analysis of the GTA VI mapping project reveals a vast, empty southwestern map section, reminiscent of how hidden off-map areas were integrated in previous games. Thirdly, a significant 2018 leak from "INSIDER GAMING"—which accurately predicted other GTA VI details like the Florida setting and a female protagonist—explicitly stated the game would be set in Vice City AND South America. Lastly, official Rockstar Games Patch Sets include patches for Jamaica and Colombia, countries not previously featured in major Rockstar titles. This, combined with the game's confirmed 1980s aesthetic (evidenced by trailer music and a leaked 'Scarface' random event), strongly suggests a narrative involving drug trafficking from Colombia to Leonida, mirroring the plot of the 1983 film. Jamaica is also posited as a potential location for drug routes or a story-driven detour. The author concludes that it is "extremely likely" players will venture beyond Leonida's shores.
