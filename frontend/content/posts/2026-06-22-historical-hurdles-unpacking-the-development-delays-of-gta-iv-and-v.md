# Historical Hurdles: Unpacking the Development Delays of GTA IV and V

Drawing insights from development documentaries, this article highlights the substantial hurdles that led to delays for both Grand Theft Auto IV and V. For GTA IV, the ambitious scope of the project—including a larger map with mountains, the simultaneous development of the RAGE engine, the transition to HD, and the intricate physics and ragdoll systems—proved far more time-consuming than initially projected. Grand Theft Auto V faced its own set of formidable challenges, primarily stemming from the technical limitations of the consoles at the time. Developers grappled with recurring internal FPS drops, frequent system crashes, and an overwhelming volume of bugs, reportedly exceeding 900,000, which ultimately necessitated the removal of much of the planned vegetation from the base game to achieve stability.

## Key Takeaways
- GTA IV's delays were attributed to an overly ambitious project scope, concurrent RAGE engine development, HD transition, and complex physics systems.
- GTA V's development was hampered by significant technical limitations of consoles, leading to persistent internal FPS drops and repeated crashes.
- Developers for GTA V reportedly had to address over 900,000 bugs during its production.
- Extensive vegetation originally planned for GTA V was removed due to technical constraints and to mitigate performance issues.
