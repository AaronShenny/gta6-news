# A Generational Divide: The Unprecedented Wait for GTA VI and Rockstar's Release Schedule

A recent Reddit post starkly illustrates the unprecedented gap between Grand Theft Auto V and the upcoming GTA VI, revealing a significant generational divide among gamers. Kids born the same year GTA V released in 2013 have literally never known a world without it, with Red Dead Redemption 2 being their only major new Rockstar experience – often when they were too young to fully appreciate it. This contrasts sharply with players born earlier, who experienced a packed decade of Rockstar releases including San Andreas, Bully, GTA IV, Red Dead Redemption, L.A. Noire, and Max Payne 3. The lengthy development cycle for GTA VI marks a dramatic shift, redefining what a 'current' GTA title means for an entire generation of players.

## Key Takeaways
- Grand Theft Auto V was released in 2013, meaning a 13-year-old today has lived their entire life with it as the current GTA.
- For this younger generation, Red Dead Redemption 2 is the only other major new Rockstar game released during their memorable gaming years.
- Older gamers, born around 2003, experienced a prolific decade of Rockstar releases prior to GTA V, including multiple GTA titles and new IPs.
- The extended wait for GTA VI highlights a dramatic slowdown in Rockstar's major game release schedule compared to previous eras.
- The article emphasizes how this long gap creates a unique generational perspective on Rockstar's output and the longevity of its flagship titles.

## FAQ
**How long has GTA V been the 'current' Grand Theft Auto title?**
GTA V was released in 2013, making it the 'current' title for over a decade, an unprecedented period for the franchise.

The article highlights a significant slowdown; earlier generations experienced many Rockstar titles (e.g., San Andreas, GTA IV, Red Dead Redemption) in a 10-year span, whereas the current generation has primarily only seen GTA V and Red Dead Redemption 2.
