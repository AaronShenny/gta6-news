# GTA 6's 2020s Setting: Will Aircraft Cockpits Finally Go Digital?

As anticipation builds for Grand Theft Auto VI, set to unfold in the 2020s, a key discussion point among the community revolves around the realism and modernity of in-game aircraft. Specifically, players are questioning whether Rockstar Games will accurately reflect contemporary aviation by incorporating modern 'glass cockpit' instrumentals, which have been prevalent since the late 1980s. There's a strong sentiment that a game set in the current decade should feature a mix of advanced digital displays and traditional analog gauges, rather than predominantly outdated designs, to maintain immersion and authenticity.

## Key Takeaways
- GTA 6 is confirmed to be set in the 2020s.
- Modern 'glass cockpits' have been a standard in aviation since the late 1980s.
- Players expect GTA 6 aircraft to feature a mix of modern glass cockpits and traditional analog gauges.
- It would feel anachronistic for most in-game aircraft in a 2020s setting to still rely solely on analog gauges.

## FAQ
**Will GTA 6 aircraft feature modern glass cockpits?**
While not officially confirmed by Rockstar, the community widely anticipates that GTA 6, being set in the 2020s, will indeed feature modern glass cockpit instrumentals in many of its aircraft, reflecting current aviation technology.

**Why is the prevalence of glass cockpits important for GTA 6?**
For many players, the inclusion of modern glass cockpits enhances the realism and immersion, aligning with the game's contemporary 2020s setting. It would feel out of place for most aircraft in a modern game to exclusively feature older analog gauges, which are less common in modern aviation.
