# GTA 6's Ultimate Test: Making Modern Life Engaging, Not Just a Big Map

In a recent discussion from the r/GTA6 subreddit, a user posited that the real measure of success for *Grand Theft Auto 6* won't be its map size or typical Rockstar hallmarks like vehicles and missions, but rather its ability to render modern life in Vice City in a way that feels genuinely alive and engaging. The user argues that while Rockstar is adept at crafting chaos and action, the more difficult task will be to integrate elements of contemporary society—such as phones, influencers, viral clips, and online drama—without turning the experience into an 'annoying parody.' The article draws a parallel to *Red Dead Redemption 2*, highlighting how that game's world achieved a profound sense of realism even during mundane moments. For *GTA 6*, the ultimate triumph would be to imbue random street scenes, interactions with NPCs, phone usage, storefronts, and quiet moments within a modern city with the same level of immersive quality. The discussion concludes by inviting readers to consider what modern life details *GTA 6* must 'get right' to achieve this.

## Key Takeaways
- GTA 6's main challenge isn't just a big map or typical Rockstar mechanics.
- The true test is making modern Vice City feel alive and authentic.
- It must avoid becoming an 'annoying parody' of modern life's elements (phones, influencers, online drama).
- Red Dead Redemption 2 is cited as an example of a world feeling real even in quiet moments.
- Success for GTA 6 means making everyday modern city details (streets, people, phones, stores) genuinely engaging.
