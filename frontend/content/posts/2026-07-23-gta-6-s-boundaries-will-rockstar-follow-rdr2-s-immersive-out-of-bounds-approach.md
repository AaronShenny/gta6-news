# GTA 6's Boundaries: Will Rockstar Follow RDR2's Immersive Out-of-Bounds Approach?

A recent discussion on Reddit speculates about how Grand Theft Auto VI will handle its 'out of bounds' areas, drawing comparisons to previous Rockstar titles. In games like Grand Theft Auto V, the map was an island, naturally preventing players from leaving the playable area. However, Red Dead Redemption 2 introduced a more immersive system where players were blocked from venturing too far, often with environmental barriers that blended into the world.

The user suggests that Rockstar might adopt RDR2's approach for GTA VI, especially given unconfirmed rumors about the GTA VI map expanding over time through story or DLC. This would align with Rockstar's potential goal of making GTA VI as immersive as RDR2. The challenge, however, lies in GTA VI's inclusion of vehicles like planes, which could easily bypass natural barriers like mountains, unlike in RDR2 where such aerial traversal is not a factor. The article raises questions about how Rockstar might implement blocking mechanisms, such as 'no-plane zones' in specific regions, to prevent players from flying too far out of bounds. While acknowledging the possibility of a return to island-based maps, the discussion primarily explores the implications of a more dynamic, RDR2-style boundary system.

## Key Takeaways
- Previous GTA games like GTA V used island maps to naturally define boundaries.
- Red Dead Redemption 2 implemented immersive out-of-bounds blocking, rather than hard barriers.
- The article speculates GTA VI might adopt RDR2's immersive boundary system, especially if the map expands over time.
- A key challenge for GTA VI's boundaries is the presence of planes, which could easily bypass RDR2-style natural barriers.
- Potential solutions include 'no-plane zones' or other creative ways to prevent players from leaving the map without resorting to an island design.
