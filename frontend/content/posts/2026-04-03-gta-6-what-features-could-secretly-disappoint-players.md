# GTA 6: What Features Could Secretly Disappoint Players?

While most discussions around Grand Theft Auto VI focus on desired features, a recent Reddit thread on r/GTA6 by user u/787Beatz shifts the conversation to 'lowkey disappointments.' The post asks players to consider features that, while seemingly positive, might actually detract from the game's experience. This includes concerns about a map that's too large and feels spread out, hyper-realism making gameplay grindy, the online mode overshadowing the story, an abundance of repetitive interiors, realistic but annoying NPCs, or an overwhelming amount of content that makes nothing feel special. The discussion also touches on the potential for the game to take itself too seriously, losing the signature chaotic GTA energy, prompting players to share their own unexpected dislikes.

## Key Takeaways
- Discussion focuses on 'lowkey disappointments' in GTA 6, not obvious flaws.
- Concerns include maps that are too big, leading to emptiness.
- Hyper-realism could make gameplay slow or 'grindy'.
- Fear of online mode once again overshadowing the single-player story.
- Too many interiors that feel 'copy-paste' and lack unique character.
- Realistic NPCs potentially being annoying rather than engaging.
- Overload of content ('too much to do') making individual activities feel less special.
- Potential for the game to lose its 'chaotic GTA energy' by taking itself too seriously.
