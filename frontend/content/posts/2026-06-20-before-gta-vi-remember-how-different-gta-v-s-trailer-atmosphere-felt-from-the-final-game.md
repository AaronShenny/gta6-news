# Before GTA VI: Remember how different GTA V's trailer atmosphere felt from the final game

As anticipation for GTA VI builds, a gaming journalist recalls how Grand Theft Auto V's initial trailers presented a world that felt significantly more "alive" and populated by "real people" due to nuanced NPC behaviors and interactions, a feeling that differed from the final game. While GTA V remains a masterpiece, the article highlights that trailers are carefully crafted to evoke a specific atmosphere. The author notes a similar trend with GTA VI, where fans are meticulously dissecting every frame of footage for minute details. This level of scrutiny, the journalist warns, could lead to inflated expectations, making it difficult for the final game to live up to the imagined perfect version and potentially causing players to focus on minor imperfections rather than the overall experience. The piece advocates for maintaining realistic expectations to ensure a more positive reception of GTA VI.

## Key Takeaways
- GTA V trailers conveyed a more "alive" world with realistic NPC behavior compared to the final game.
- The difference wasn't about graphics but the "feeling of life" and nuanced NPC interactions.
- Trailers are carefully directed to create specific atmospheres, which may not fully reflect the final product.
- Current GTA VI hype involves intense frame-by-frame analysis of early footage by fans.
- Over-analysis and inflated expectations for GTA VI could lead to disappointment upon release.
- The author advises keeping expectations realistic to better appreciate the final game and avoid focusing on minor imperfections.
