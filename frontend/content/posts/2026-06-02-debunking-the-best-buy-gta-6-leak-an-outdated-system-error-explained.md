# Debunking the Best Buy GTA 6 'Leak': An Outdated System Error Explained

A recent 'leak' from Best Buy displaying a potential release date for Grand Theft Auto 6 caused a stir among fans, but a Reddit post offers a logical explanation: the date was likely an outdated placeholder for a pre-order window. The author argues that pre-orders, especially for Rockstar titles, are rarely offered more than three months in advance. Instead, the 'leaked' date probably reflected an initial pre-order period set for approximately a week before the game's original, now-delayed release. Best Buy's automated systems simply failed to update this date, leading to the confusion. The actual pre-order window, if reflective of a week before the current target release, would be closer to early November.

## Key Takeaways
- The Best Buy 'leak' of a GTA 6 date was likely an outdated pre-order placeholder, not a true release date leak.
- Pre-orders for major titles, particularly from Rockstar Games, are typically not offered more than three months in advance.
- The displayed date was probably intended for a pre-order window roughly a week before the game's initial, now-delayed release.
- Best Buy's automated systems reportedly failed to update this date after GTA 6 experienced a delay.
- An updated pre-order window, based on the 'week before release' model, would align with a date around November 8th-11th, according to the author.
