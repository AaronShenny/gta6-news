# GTA 6 Predicted to Rival Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas in Scope and Depth

A gaming journalist believes that Grand Theft Auto 6 is poised to be a significant competitor to GTA San Andreas, arguing that its design philosophy leans heavily into the strengths of both San Andreas and Red Dead Redemption 2. Based on current leaks and official trailers, the author highlights numerous features that suggest a return to a more grounded, immersive, and player-agency-driven experience, moving away from the style of GTA V. These include extensive customization, diverse exploration, a loyalty-driven narrative, and a rich array of activities reminiscent of the series' most acclaimed entries.

## Key Takeaways
- GTA 6 is expected to feature an expanded variety of restaurants and shops, surpassing what was seen in San Andreas and GTA IV.
- The map's 'tiny islands' nature, similar to original Vice City and San Andreas, will create a sense of vastness and challenging navigation.
- The map will offer diverse, populated areas for exploration, with more myths and world events than San Andreas and RDR2.
- Classic GTA interactions and minigames, including combat training (like 'Fight Club' for skill improvement) and activities from GTA V, are set to return.
- The story is anticipated to be more grounded and loyalty-driven, akin to San Andreas and RDR2, focusing on relationships.
- Features like breaking into houses and a progressive map unlock system, similar to San Andreas, are suggested to return.
- Character customization, including gyms and weight changes (fat/muscle), has been hinted at, reminiscent of San Andreas.
- Eating mechanics are likely to be re-implemented, potentially alongside RDR2-style inventory and environmental elements like sun lotion.
- Trailer music has evoked strong San Andreas nostalgia, suggesting a similar overall vibe and atmosphere for the game.
- GTA 6 is seen as drawing inspiration from RDR2, which itself felt like a 'Western San Andreas' to the author.
- Extensive character customization for Lucia and Jason, allowing for diverse appearances, is expected to rival San Andreas' depth.
- The game is predicted to feature a 'rags-to-riches' progression, starting with humble beginnings and working your way up, similar to previous GTA titles.
- The ability to rent hotels/motels and potentially buy multiple houses, a feature from San Andreas, is suggested to return.
- Location-specific special vehicle findings and car part pickups will encourage thorough map exploration, echoing San Andreas' design.
