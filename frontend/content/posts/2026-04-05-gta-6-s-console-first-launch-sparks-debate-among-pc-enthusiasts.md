# GTA 6's Console-First Launch Sparks Debate Among PC Enthusiasts

The impending launch of Grand Theft Auto VI, widely anticipated to be a console-first release, is already generating significant discussion and a degree of frustration within the PC gaming community. A Reddit user, u/Hollowbird123, articulated a common sentiment, highlighting the predicament for PC players who, despite owning powerful systems (e.g., Ryzen 7 5800x, RX 6800XT), may need to purchase a console that will be six years old by GTA VI's release. Unable to afford a potential PS5 Pro, the user is considering a used Xbox Series X and is seeking community expectations regarding the game's performance on that console, specifically at 1440p. This scenario underscores a broader concern among PC gamers about delayed releases and the perceived compromise on visual fidelity and frame rates when forced onto older console generations.

## Key Takeaways
- GTA 6 is broadly expected to launch on consoles exclusively, with a PC version likely to follow later.
- Current-generation consoles (PS5, Xbox Series X/S) will be approximately six years old when GTA 6 releases.
- PC gamers with high-end rigs are contemplating purchasing an aging console to play the game at launch.
- Concerns have been raised regarding GTA 6's potential performance on the Xbox Series X, particularly at 1440p.
- The cost of newer console iterations, such as a rumored PS5 Pro, is a financial consideration for some players.

## FAQ
**Will GTA 6 be released on PC at launch?**
Based on community expectations and Rockstar's historical release patterns, GTA 6 is widely anticipated to launch on consoles first, with a PC version likely to be released at a later, unconfirmed date.

**How old will current-gen consoles be when GTA 6 releases?**
The Xbox Series X/S and PlayStation 5 will be approximately six years old by the time Grand Theft Auto VI is released, leading to discussions about their graphical capabilities.

**What are PC gamers' main concerns about GTA 6's console exclusivity?**
PC gamers are primarily concerned about the necessity of purchasing an 'aging' console to play the game, potential compromises in graphical fidelity and performance compared to a high-end PC, and the anticipated waiting period for a PC port.

**Is the Xbox Series X expected to run GTA 6 at 1440p?**
While the Xbox Series X is capable of 1440p output, specific performance details for GTA 6 are currently unknown. The community is speculating on the frame rates and graphical settings the game might achieve on the console at that resolution.
