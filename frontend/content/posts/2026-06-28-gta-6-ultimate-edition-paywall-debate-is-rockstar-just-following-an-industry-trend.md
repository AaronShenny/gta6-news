# GTA 6 Ultimate Edition 'Paywall' Debate: Is Rockstar Just Following an Industry Trend?

A recent online discussion, stemming from a Reddit post, addresses the controversy surrounding content locked behind ultimate editions for Grand Theft Auto 6. The author argues that the rumored GTA 6 ultimate edition bonuses—such as cosmetics, a few cars, and a side mission featuring classic cars—are not unprecedented. They draw parallels to Grand Theft Auto V's 2013 ultimate edition, which offered similar in-game items like garages, exclusive cars, clothes, and online character options, only for these to become free in the PS4 remaster. The post asserts that this practice is not a 'paywall' for the base game but rather additional content, a common strategy seen across major franchises like Call of Duty, 2K, WWE (locking playable characters), and Assassin's Creed (locking missions). The author specifically references Mass Effect 3's 'From Ashes' DLC, which locked significant lore and a crew member behind a special edition or an extra purchase on day one. The piece concludes that Rockstar is not innovating 'scummy' behavior but rather continuing an industry trend that players have tacitly accepted over many years. A separate, strong sentiment expressed is the perceived 'insanity' of potentially not having a physical edition for the game.

## Key Takeaways
- GTA 6 ultimate edition bonuses are largely cosmetic and additional content, similar to GTA V's 2013 ultimate edition offerings.
- GTA V's original ultimate edition content was later made free in its PS4 remaster.
- The author argues this is not a 'paywall' for the base game but rather an industry-wide practice for nearly two decades.
- Numerous other major game franchises (Call of Duty, 2K, WWE, Assassin's Creed, Mass Effect 3) have historically locked content behind special editions or DLC.
- Rockstar Games is not seen as the originator of this 'scummy' behavior, but rather a participant in an established industry trend.
- A significant point of contention raised is the potential absence of a physical edition for GTA 6, which the author finds 'insane'.

## FAQ
**Are GTA 6 ultimate edition bonuses locking essential game content?**
According to the article, the bonuses are primarily cosmetic and additional content, not essential to the core gameplay experience, aligning with past GTA titles and other games.

**Has Rockstar engaged in this practice before?**
Yes, GTA V's 2013 ultimate edition offered similar exclusive in-game items, though these were later made free in the PS4 remaster.

**Is this content-locking practice unique to Rockstar?**
No, the article argues this is a widespread industry practice, citing examples from Call of Duty, 2K, WWE, Assassin's Creed, and Mass Effect 3, among others.

**What is the main criticism mentioned regarding GTA 6, aside from the ultimate edition bonuses?**
The article highlights strong disapproval regarding the potential absence of a physical edition for GTA 6.
