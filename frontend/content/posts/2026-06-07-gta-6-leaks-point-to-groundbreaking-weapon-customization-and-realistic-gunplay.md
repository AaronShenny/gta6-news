# GTA 6 Leaks Point to Groundbreaking Weapon Customization and Realistic Gunplay

Recent leaks from GTA 6 suggest a significantly deeper weapon customization system compared to GTA 5, with options ranging from scopes and foregrips to different barrel lengths, stocks, magazines, and even cerakote-style coatings. The article speculates on the inclusion of laser attachments and a more realistic caliber-based ammo system (e.g., 9mm vs .40, hollow points vs FMJ) rather than simplified ammo types. Further discussions include the potential for detailed holster mechanics and drawing animations, as well as NPC behavior that reflects regional gun laws, particularly in the Leonida (Florida) setting, where a pedestrian observed jumping into a shootout hints at enhanced environmental realism. Rumors also circulate about user-generated content, possibly allowing players to create or 3D-print custom weapon parts and designs, pushing the boundaries of player agency in weapon development.

## Key Takeaways
- Extensive weapon customization hinted by leaks, including scopes, foregrips, barrel lengths, stocks, magazines, flashlights, switches, and detailed color/coating options.
- Desire for a caliber-based ammo system (e.g., 9mm, .40, .45) with specific bullet types (hollow points, FMJ, +P) for added depth.
- Multiple pistol models and sizes expected, leading to more realistic handling and performance differences.
- Speculation on detailed holster mechanics (IWB/OWB, carry positions) and accurate drawing animations.
- NPC behavior potentially reflecting regional gun laws, with instances like pedestrians engaging in firefights in Leonida (Florida) noted as a 'Florida-coded' realism detail.
- Rumors of user-generated content for weapons, possibly allowing players to create or 3D-print custom guns and aftermarket parts.
