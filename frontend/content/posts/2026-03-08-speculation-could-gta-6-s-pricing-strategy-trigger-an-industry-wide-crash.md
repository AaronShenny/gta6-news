# Speculation: Could GTA 6's Pricing Strategy Trigger an Industry-Wide Crash?

A recent post on Reddit's r/GTA6 community offers a speculative, pessimistic outlook on the future of the AAA video game industry, centered around the anticipated release of GTA 6. The author posits that while GTA 6 might 'barely get away with' potentially high pricing, its success could inadvertently set a dangerous new norm. They predict that other AAA developers will follow suit, attempting to charge $100+ for their titles. However, unlike GTA, these games are expected to 'giga-flop' due to a lack of comparable appeal, triggering a 'slow excruciating AAA vidya crash.' This crash is projected to result in dozens of studio closures and a prolonged downturn in the video game economy, with recovery not expected until after a related 'AI crash' has also run its course. In essence, the author foresees a significantly worse economic landscape for gaming for years to come.

## Key Takeaways
- GTA 6 may succeed with high prices, but with lower-than-expected internal profits.

- The crash could lead to dozens of video game studios shuttering.

- The overall video game economy is projected to worsen significantly for several years.
