# GTA 6: The Great FPS Debate - 60 FPS or Enhanced World Detail at 30 FPS?

A recent discussion on the r/GTA6 subreddit, initiated by user u/No_Underscore12, has ignited a familiar debate among fans: **what is the optimal performance target for Grand Theft Auto 6?** The user posed a direct question to the community, asking whether players would prefer a 'smooth 60 FPS' or a 'smooth 30 FPS with more graphical and atmospheric detail, more vehicle/NPC density, and a higher view distance'.

The original poster personally leans towards the latter, stating that 30 FPS is 'less noticeable on third-person games like GTA' and even less so when using a controller. They further drew a comparison to Red Dead Redemption 2, a game widely praised for its visuals despite being locked at 30 FPS, noting they 'didn’t even realize it was locked to 30 till half way through the story'. This sentiment highlights a common divide in the gaming community, where some prioritize visual fidelity and world immersion over raw frame rate, especially in narrative-driven, open-world titles.
