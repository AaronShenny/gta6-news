# GTA 6: Community Hopes for Extended, More Chaotic 'Wasted' Cutscenes

A recent post on the GTA 6 subreddit, submitted by /u/Professional-Toe-879, has ignited a conversation about a specific design choice for the highly anticipated game: the length of 'Wasted' (death) cutscenes. The user expressed dissatisfaction with GTA 5's death sequences, noting they were often too short and would prematurely cut off, particularly during dramatic events like high-speed crashes or falls from great heights. The core suggestion is for GTA 6 to feature extended 'Wasted' scenes, allowing the chaos and immediate aftermath of a character's demise to play out for a longer duration, ideally until the character is completely stationary, before fading out. This, the user believes, would significantly enhance the fun and immersion, prompting others in the community to share their thoughts on the matter.

## Key Takeaways
- GTA 5's 'Wasted' cutscenes were criticized for being too short.
- Scenes often loaded prematurely, cutting off chaotic moments like high-speed crashes.
- The community hopes for extended death sequences in GTA 6.
- Suggestion to let scenes play out until the character is completely stationary.
- The proposed change aims to enhance player enjoyment and immersion.

## FAQ
**What is the main suggestion for GTA 6's 'Wasted' scenes?**
The primary suggestion is for GTA 6 to feature longer and more dynamic 'Wasted' (death) cutscenes, contrasting them with the perceived brevity and premature fade-outs of GTA 5's sequences.

The user believes longer scenes would allow the immediate chaos of a character's death to play out more fully, enhancing the fun and immersion, especially during dramatic incidents like crashes or falls.
