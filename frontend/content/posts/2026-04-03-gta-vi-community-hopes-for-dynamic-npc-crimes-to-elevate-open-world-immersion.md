# GTA VI Community Hopes for Dynamic NPC Crimes to Elevate Open-World Immersion

A compelling discussion has emerged from the r/GTA6 subreddit, where user /u/Venomesis articulated a popular desire among fans for Grand Theft Auto VI to feature dynamic, AI-driven NPC criminal activities. Drawing inspiration from the Watch Dogs series, the user posits that allowing non-player characters to independently commit crimes—such as shootouts or gas station robberies—would make the game world feel significantly more alive and less solely focused on the player. This enhancement, they argue, would foster a more unpredictable and engaging free-roaming experience, potentially leading to organic, large-scale police responses, contrasting with GTA V's more limited dynamic events. The sentiment suggests a strong belief that improved NPC simulation will be a key factor in making GTA VI's single-player exploration truly captivating.

## Key Takeaways
- Fans advocate for dynamic NPC crimes in GTA VI.
- Inspiration drawn from the Watch Dogs series' dynamic events.
- Goal: create a more 'alive' and less player-centric open world.
- Envisioned scenarios include large-scale police pursuits and SWAT responses to NPC actions.
- Highlights limitations in GTA V's current dynamic event system.
- Belief that enhanced NPC simulation will significantly improve single-player free roaming.
