# GTA 6's Dedicated 'Frame Friday' Series Hits 100th Post, Teases Imminent Trailer 3 and Post-Launch Future

The long-running community series 'Frame Friday,' spearheaded by Reddit user /u/Denso95, has reached its 100th milestone, celebrating two and a half years of in-depth analysis and speculation surrounding Grand Theft Auto VI's trailers and official content. The series, which began in December 2023 with the first trailer, adapted its format to accommodate the influx of content from the second trailer. Despite mixed feedback, the initiative has fostered a dedicated community. Looking ahead, /u/Denso95 anticipates the imminent release of Trailer 3, promising to increase the scale of future posts to 10-20 frames, and expresses plans to continue the series with user-generated in-game screenshots even after GTA 6's launch, provided community interest remains high.

## Key Takeaways
- The 'Frame Friday' series on Reddit has reached its 100th post, celebrating two and a half years of Grand Theft Auto VI content analysis.
- The series, led by /u/Denso95, began on December 22, 2023, initially covering Trailer 1 and later expanding its scope for Trailer 2.
- The community initiative has garnered significant engagement, fostering discussion and speculation among GTA 6 fans.
- With official content from Rockstar Games nearly exhausted, the host anticipates that Trailer 3 is 'very close' to release.
- Future 'Frame Friday' posts will increase in scale, analyzing 10-20 frames at a time to cover new content efficiently.
- Plans are in place to continue the series even after GTA 6's release, with the host proposing to analyze user-submitted in-game screenshots if community feedback remains positive.

**Will 'Frame Friday' continue after GTA 6 is released?**
Yes, the host plans to continue the series by analyzing user-submitted in-game screenshots after the game's launch, provided there is positive community feedback.
