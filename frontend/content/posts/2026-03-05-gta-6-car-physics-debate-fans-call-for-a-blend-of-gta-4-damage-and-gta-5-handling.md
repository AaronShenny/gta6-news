# GTA 6 Car Physics Debate: Fans Call for a Blend of GTA 4 Damage and GTA 5 Handling

A recent discussion on the GTA 6 subreddit has brought the perennial debate over car physics and damage models back into the spotlight. User /u/Western-Stress1185 expressed a desire for Grand Theft Auto 6 to strike a unique balance, specifically advocating for the robust, visually satisfying car damage reminiscent of GTA 4, coupled with the more accessible and arcade-style handling introduced in GTA 5. The user recalled GTA 4's more realistic vehicle physics – characterized by heavy vehicles, a propensity for rolling, and challenging high-speed turns – which were reportedly simplified in GTA 5 following player feedback. While appreciating GTA 4's destructive crash aesthetics, the user also noted GTA 5's fleeting feature of driving on three wheels on last-gen consoles. Acknowledging the complexities of game development, the user's ultimate wish is for a car experience in GTA 6 that isn't 'too arcady but also not too realistic,' offering a fun yet visually impactful driving experience.

## Key Takeaways
- Reddit user proposes a hybrid car physics model for GTA 6.
- Desire for GTA 4's realistic and visually destructive car damage.
- Preference for GTA 5's more arcade-style and accessible car handling.
- Recalls GTA 4's heavier, more challenging physics and GTA 5's simplified approach.
- Aims for a balanced driving experience, neither overly arcade nor overly realistic.
