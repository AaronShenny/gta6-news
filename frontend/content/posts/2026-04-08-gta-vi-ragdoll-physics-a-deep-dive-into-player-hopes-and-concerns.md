# GTA VI Ragdoll Physics: A Deep Dive into Player Hopes and Concerns

A dedicated GTA VI fan has sparked discussion within the community regarding their hopes and concerns for the game's ragdoll physics. Citing a preference for the more dynamic and realistic reactions seen in GTA IV, the player expresses disappointment with GTA V's 'hollow' NPC physics and even notes instances in Red Dead Redemption 2 where physics seemed to be sacrificed for animation smoothness. With GTA VI being built for current-gen hardware, there's a strong desire to see Rockstar's Euphoria engine pushed to its limits, delivering active reactions, true momentum-based impacts, and a genuine sense of weight for in-game characters. While the trailer offered a glimpse of promising physics, the community remains cautiously optimistic, questioning whether GTA VI will lean towards the chaotic realism of GTA IV or the more controlled, animation-heavy style of RDR2.

## Key Takeaways
- Concerns that GTA V's ragdoll physics were a step back from GTA IV, feeling 'hollow'.
- RDR2 improved weight and brutality, but occasionally sacrificed physics for smooth animations.
- Hopes for GTA VI to push the Euphoria engine to its limits on current-gen hardware.
- Specific desires include active reactions (catching falls, grabbing ledges), real momentum-based impacts, and NPCs feeling like 'living beings' with true weight.
- Trailer footage, like a character falling through a table, looks promising but could be scripted.
- Community discussion revolves around whether GTA VI will return to GTA IV's reactive physics or RDR2's controlled style.

## FAQ
**What are the main concerns about GTA VI's ragdoll physics?**
The primary concern is that GTA VI might not fully leverage its current-gen capabilities to deliver realistic, dynamic ragdoll physics, potentially opting for a more animation-driven or 'controlled' feel, similar to what some players perceived in GTA V or even RDR2 at times.

**Which previous Rockstar game's physics are players hoping GTA VI will emulate?**
Many players are hoping GTA VI will lean back into the more reactive, chaotic, and realistic physics seen in Grand Theft Auto IV, where NPCs displayed more dynamic reactions to force and environmental interactions.

**What specific improvements are players looking for in GTA VI's ragdoll physics?**
Players are looking for active reactions (e.g., NPCs trying to catch themselves or grab objects), reactions based on real momentum and impact points rather than pre-set 'death poses,' and a true sense of weight, making NPCs feel like living beings rather than 'bags of flour'.

**Did the GTA VI trailer offer any clues about the physics?**
The trailer showed some promising physics, such as a character falling through a table. However, players are cautious, noting that such moments could be scripted for cinematic effect and may not represent general gameplay physics.
