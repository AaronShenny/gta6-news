# GTA 6 Inventory Challenge: Fans Debate How Rockstar Will Handle Non-Weapon Items

As excitement builds for Grand Theft Auto 6, the dedicated fanbase is meticulously dissecting every potential game mechanic, including the often-overlooked inventory system for non-weapon items. A recent discussion on the r/GTA6 subreddit, initiated by user u/DaddyPattyBatman, ignited a debate surrounding how players will manage items such as food, healing kits, binoculars, and fishing poles. Participants quickly dismissed the Red Dead Redemption 2-esque satchel as unlikely for GTA 6's urban environment and found the concept of relying solely on a car trunk inconvenient for exploration in remote areas. The core of the discussion revolves around finding a realistic and convenient solution that avoids both an ever-present backpack and the unrealistic capacity of character pockets, prompting speculation on Rockstar's innovative approach to this crucial aspect of gameplay.

## Key Takeaways
- GTA 6 fans are actively speculating on the game's non-weapon inventory system.
- The discussion focuses on items like food, healing supplies, binoculars, and fishing poles.
- The RDR2 satchel model is generally considered unsuitable for GTA 6's setting and aesthetic.
- Using only a car trunk for storage is deemed impractical for remote exploration.
- The community seeks a realistic and convenient inventory solution that avoids unrealistic pocket capacity or a constant backpack.
