# GTA 6 Story Mode Economy Needs to Keep Money Meaningful, Says Fan

A Grand Theft Auto fan has voiced a common sentiment regarding GTA V's story mode: the eventual pointlessness of money. The article highlights how, after completing major heists, players are often left with millions of dollars and little meaningful to spend it on, lacking recurring expenses or significant long-term financial goals. The author expresses a strong desire for GTA 6 to introduce a much deeper, more engaging economy in single-player. This would include expensive luxury purchases, businesses requiring ongoing investment, and maintenance costs for properties and vehicles, ensuring that earning money remains a rewarding and purposeful activity throughout the entire game, rather than just in GTA Online.

## Key Takeaways
- GTA V's story mode made money pointless after the main heists due to a lack of meaningful spending options.
- Players accumulated millions with no significant recurring expenses like rent, mortgages, or business upkeep.
- The author hopes GTA 6 introduces a deeper single-player economy with expensive long-term goals.
- Desired features include luxury homes, businesses requiring investment, and maintenance costs for properties and vehicles.
- This would ensure earning money remains rewarding and purposeful throughout the entire game.
- The article argues against the idea that deep economies are only for GTA Online, citing reasons like player preference for single-player, internet access, and avoiding griefers.
- Rockstar should bring some of the attention given to GTA Online's progression systems to the story mode economy.

## FAQ
**What was the main issue with money in GTA V's story mode?**
Money became pointless after the main heists because there were few meaningful ways to spend large sums, and no recurring expenses to manage.

**What does the author hope to see in GTA 6's economy?**
A deeper economy with expensive long-term goals like luxury properties, businesses requiring investment, and recurring maintenance costs to make earning money continuously rewarding.

**Why does the author believe this isn't just a feature for GTA Online?**
Many players prefer single-player experiences, may have limited internet, or wish to avoid online griefers, making a robust story mode economy essential for them.
