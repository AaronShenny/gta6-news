# Why 'GTA 6 will play like GTA 5.3' is a Delusional Statement

The assertion that Grand Theft Auto 6 will merely be a slightly improved version of GTA 5 (dubbed 'GTA 5.3') is dismissed as delusional. The article argues that given Rockstar's track record, particularly with Red Dead Redemption 2's unparalleled detail and dynamism, coupled with significantly larger development teams, budgets, extended production times, and the power of next-gen consoles alongside advanced AI tools, GTA 6 is poised to be an unprecedented technological and experiential leap. It's positioned as Rockstar's next long-term 'cash cow,' implying maximum effort and innovation.

## Key Takeaways
- Red Dead Redemption 2 (RDR2) still surpasses 90% of open-world games in terms of mastercrafted map, visual effects, sound design, story depth, and dynamic NPC interactions.
- GTA 6 boasts a development team three times the size of RDR2's, with an eight-year development cycle (compared to RDR2's five years), and an almost limitless budget.
- Next-generation console capabilities (e.g., PS5's SSD, processing power, double RAM) enable significant advancements, including HDR and ray tracing.
- Rockstar now utilizes advanced AI toolsets to enhance development efficiency and refinement.
- The belief that GTA 6 will be a minor upgrade misunderstands the scale of ambition for a game intended to be a flagship title for the next two decades.
