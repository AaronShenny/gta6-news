# Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 4 - Release Date, Story, Platforms & Everything We Know

Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 4, led by series creator Infinity Ward, is set to launch on October 23, 2026. This highly anticipated title aims to revitalize the franchise, following a challenging period for recent Call of Duty releases. MW4 will be available across PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X|S, PC, and notably, the Nintendo Switch 2, marking Call of Duty's return to Nintendo platforms since 2013. The game will not be a day-one Game Pass title, with availability expected roughly a year post-launch.

The campaign takes players to the Korean peninsula, following Private Park's "zero-to-hero" journey, with Captain Price making a return in a new capacity. Multiplayer promises a "grounded" experience, emphasizing authentic gunplay with significant updates to mechanics like bullet trajectory, weapon motion, and visual fidelity, alongside 12 new core 6v6 maps at launch. The extraction-based DMZ mode is also confirmed to return. While an open beta is planned, specific details are yet to be revealed. Despite its robust feature set, industry analysts do not expect Modern Warfare 4 to outsell Rockstar's Grand Theft Auto 6, which launches a month later. Activision has also acknowledged its use of generative AI in Call of Duty content, though specific applications for MW4 remain unconfirmed.

## Key Takeaways
- **Release Date:** October 23, 2026.
- **Developer:** Infinity Ward leads development, supported by numerous Activision studios.
- **Platforms:** PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X|S, PC, and Nintendo Switch 2 (native version).
- **Game Pass:** Not available on day one; expected after approximately one year.
- **Campaign:** Set on the Korean peninsula, starring Private Park, with Captain Price returning. Features global locations.
- **Multiplayer:** Promises a "grounded" feel, "most authentic" gunplay, 12 new 6v6 maps, Gunfight, and Big War modes.
- **DMZ Mode:** The extraction-based mode from MWII is confirmed to return.
- **Sales Expectations:** Not predicted to outsell GTA 6, which releases a month later.
- **AI Usage:** Activision confirms using generative AI in CoD development, but specifics for MW4 are unknown.
