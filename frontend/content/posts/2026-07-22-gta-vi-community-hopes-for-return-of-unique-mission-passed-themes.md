# GTA VI: Community Hopes for Return of Unique 'Mission Passed' Themes

A dedicated Grand Theft Auto fan, u/LordWeilheim, has shared a heartfelt wish for **GTA VI** to bring back a subtle yet impactful feature from **GTA IV**: unique 'Mission Passed' themes. The user argues that these distinct musical cues, which varied from mission to mission in GTA IV, significantly enhanced the memorability and identity of each completed task, making the reward feel more genuine and the overall atmosphere richer.

In stark contrast, **GTA V** adopted a single, generic 'Mission Passed' theme. While iconic in its own right, the user believes its constant repetition across nearly every mission made the experience repetitive and somewhat bland compared to the older titles. The sentiment is that while it's a small detail, the reintroduction of multiple, tailored 'Mission Passed' themes would be instrumental in making each mission in GTA VI feel truly unique and memorable, contributing greatly to the game's immersive quality.

## Key Takeaways
- A Reddit user desires the return of unique 'Mission Passed' themes in GTA VI.
- GTA IV featured different themes for various missions, making them more memorable.
- GTA V used a single, generic 'Mission Passed' theme, which felt repetitive.
- Unique themes are seen as a small detail that greatly enhances mission identity and player reward.

**What is the main feature a Reddit user hopes returns in GTA VI?**
The main feature a Reddit user, u/LordWeilheim, hopes returns in GTA VI is the implementation of unique 'Mission Passed' themes for different missions, similar to how it was in GTA IV.

In GTA IV, different missions had distinct 'Mission Passed' themes, contributing to their unique identity. In contrast, GTA V featured only one generic 'Mission Passed' theme that played after almost every mission.

This feature is considered important because it made each mission feel more memorable and rewarding in GTA IV, adding to the overall atmosphere. The lack of variety in GTA V made mission completion feel repetitive and bland.
