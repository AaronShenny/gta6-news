# GTA 6 Confirmed to Launch as Digital-Only 'Code-in-a-Box' Release, Fueling Industry Shift Debate

The gaming industry is witnessing a significant shift towards digital-only releases, a trend highlighted by Sony's recent announcement to cease PlayStation disc production by 2028. In line with this controversial movement, Rockstar Games has reportedly confirmed plans for *Grand Theft Auto 6* to be a 'code-in-a-box' release, meaning the highly anticipated title will not include a physical disc. This decision places *GTA 6* squarely in the midst of a growing debate among gamers and industry professionals regarding the future of physical media. Fans of physical games express concerns over ownership, game preservation, and the impact on second-hand markets and retailers like GameStop, which traditionally rely on physical sales. The move by Rockstar, alongside Sony's broader digital push and the closure of older PlayStation storefronts, reinforces the industry's direction, prompting a strong reaction from the community defending physical media and lamenting the perceived loss of true game ownership.

## Key Takeaways
- Rockstar Games is planning a 'code-in-a-box' release for GTA 6, meaning no physical disc will be available.
- This decision aligns with a broader industry trend towards digital-only distribution, exemplified by Sony ending PlayStation disc production by 2028.
- The move has fueled concerns among gamers about game ownership, preservation, and the future of physical retail and second-hand markets.
- GTA 6's digital-only approach is seen by some as another sign of the 'glum' state of the gaming industry.
