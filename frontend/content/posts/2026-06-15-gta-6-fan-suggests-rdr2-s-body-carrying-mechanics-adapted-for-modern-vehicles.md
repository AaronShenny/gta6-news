# GTA 6: Fan Suggests RDR2's Body-Carrying Mechanics Adapted for Modern Vehicles

A passionate Grand Theft Auto VI fan has voiced a unique request for the upcoming game, suggesting it adopt and evolve a popular mechanic from Red Dead Redemption 2: the ability to carry NPCs, dead or alive. Drawing parallels to RDR2's freedom in hogtying, dragging, or disposing of bodies, the user envisions GTA VI allowing players to perform similar actions, but adapted for a modern setting. Specifically, they hope for the capability to store NPCs in car trunks for transport across the map, believing this would significantly enhance the game's sandbox potential and offer 'insane, modern ways' to interact with the world.

## Key Takeaways
- User suggests GTA 6 incorporate Red Dead Redemption 2's body-carrying mechanics.
- RDR2 allowed players to hogtie, drag, feed to predators, and toss NPCs off cliffs.
- The fan proposes adapting this feature for GTA 6's modern setting, utilizing vehicles.
- A key suggestion is the ability to store NPCs (dead or alive) in car trunks for transport.
- The user believes this would provide an 'ultimate sandbox experience' with expanded freedom.
