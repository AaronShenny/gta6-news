# GTA 6 Launch to Miss Path Tracing, Sparking Fan Disappointment

A recent Reddit post reflects a sentiment of disappointment among PC gaming enthusiasts regarding the upcoming release of Grand Theft Auto 6, specifically its expected absence of path tracing at launch. The original poster emphasizes that path tracing significantly enhances visual realism, making games look more lifelike. However, they acknowledge that current and even future console hardware, including the hypothetical PlayStation 6, may not possess the necessary power to run such a demanding graphical feature, especially given the prevalence of AMD GPUs in consoles, making it primarily a PC-exclusive setting. While lamenting the game's minimum two-year console exclusivity, the author expresses hope that the eventual PC release will offer 'unlocked graphics settings,' hinting at the potential for advanced visual options like path tracing on that platform.

## Key Takeaways
- GTA 6 is not expected to feature path tracing upon its initial release.
- Path tracing is noted for significantly improving game realism and visual quality.
- The absence is attributed to the perceived hardware limitations of current and future consoles, particularly with AMD GPUs.
- Path tracing is described as a 'PC exclusive graphical setting'.
- GTA 6 will be a console exclusive for a minimum of two years.
- PC players anticipate 'unlocked graphics settings' upon the game's eventual PC release, implying potential for advanced features.
