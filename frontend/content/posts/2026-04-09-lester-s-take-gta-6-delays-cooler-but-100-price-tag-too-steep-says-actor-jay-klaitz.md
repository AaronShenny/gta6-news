# Lester's Take: GTA 6 Delays 'Cooler,' But $100 Price Tag Too Steep, Says Actor Jay Klaitz

Jay Klaitz, the actor behind Grand Theft Auto V's Lester Crest, has shared further insights on Grand Theft Auto 6, suggesting that additional delays could ultimately make the game 'cooler' by allowing developers the necessary time for polish. While he previously acknowledged that GTA 6's extensive content might warrant a $100 price, Klaitz now personally states he wouldn't pay that much, hoping instead for a more 'accessible' price point for all players. He even joked that his character, Lester, would price the game low to lure players into spending more on GTA Online.

The highly anticipated title has faced multiple delays, moving from an initial Fall 2025 window to May 2026, and most recently to November 19, 2026. Rockstar Games officially cited the need for more time to achieve the expected level of polish. A marketing push slated for this summer is expected to signal the stability of the latest release date.

The discussion around a potential $100 price tag for GTA 6 originated from researcher Matthew Ball, who noted in January 2025 that, adjusted for inflation, past GTA titles like GTA 4 ($90) and GTA 5 ($83) were already significantly priced. Ball highlighted that while other consumer goods and services have seen price increases, video games have remained relatively low. His personal prediction for GTA 6's price is $70.

Klaitz, a Florida native, also mused about GTA 6's setting of Leonida (a stand-in for Florida), wondering if Rockstar might include its own legally distinct version of a major attraction, such as a 'Psycho Park' akin to Walt Disney World.
