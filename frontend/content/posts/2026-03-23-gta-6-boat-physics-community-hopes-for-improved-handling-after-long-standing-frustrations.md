# GTA 6 Boat Physics: Community Hopes for Improved Handling After Long-Standing Frustrations

A recent Reddit discussion has brought to light a persistent point of contention among Grand Theft Auto players: the series' boat physics. User u/irshadtriton initiated a thread expressing their strong dislike for current boat handling, specifically pointing out that vessels frequently catch air, leading to a frustrating loss of momentum upon impact. The original poster also criticized the audio experience, describing an annoying flicker between high-pitched engine revs while airborne and muffled sounds when hitting the water. These issues reportedly lead players to actively avoid using boats in the game, sparking a wider community conversation about expectations for mechanical and physics changes in the upcoming GTA 6.

## Key Takeaways
- Long-standing player dissatisfaction with boat handling in the GTA series.
- Boats frequently catch air due to waves and speed.
- Significant loss of momentum when boats are airborne.
- Frustrating audio flickering between high-pitched revs in the air and muffled sounds on water.
- These issues lead some players to avoid using boats altogether.
- Community discussion on desired improvements for boat physics in GTA 6.

## FAQ
**What are the main complaints about boat physics in GTA?**
Players primarily dislike how boats constantly catch air, leading to a noticeable loss of momentum when they hit the water, and the disruptive flickering audio between airborne and submerged states.

**How do current boat physics impact player experience?**
The frustrating handling, momentum loss, and audio glitches often deter players from using boats, limiting their engagement with the game's water-based activities.

**What are players hoping for in GTA 6 regarding boat mechanics?**
The community is looking for significant improvements to boat physics and handling, aiming for a more realistic and enjoyable watercraft experience that addresses current issues like airborne momentum and audio.
