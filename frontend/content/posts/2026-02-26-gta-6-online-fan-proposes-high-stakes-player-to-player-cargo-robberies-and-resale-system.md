# GTA 6 Online: Fan Proposes High-Stakes Player-to-Player Cargo Robberies and Resale System

A passionate Grand Theft Auto fan has shared an intriguing concept for GTA 6 Online, advocating for a robust player-versus-player (PvP) system centered around illicit cargo and drug shipments. The core idea proposes that players should be able to actively rob and then resell other players' goods for a significant profit, transforming routine delivery missions into thrilling, high-stakes encounters. This departs from GTA V's current model, which often incentivizes cargo destruction for minimal reward. To ensure fairness and strategic depth, the proposal suggests that sell missions should not be globally broadcast. Instead, intelligence gathering would be dynamic, relying on in-game social networks, bribing informants, or simply observing suspicious activity. Furthermore, robbed players would have a chance to recover their stolen goods, and a more extreme, post-sale money laundering mechanic is also floated to add another layer of risk. Crucially, the system would hinge on skill-based hijacking and the absence of overpowered futuristic vehicles, ensuring a balanced and engaging experience that promises to make online sessions far more intense and exciting.

## Key Takeaways
- Cargo and drug shipments in GTA 6 Online should be robbable by other players and resellable for profit.
- Sell missions should not be automatically pinged to the entire lobby; intelligence should be gathered through in-game networks or observation.
- Robbed players should have an opportunity to recover their stolen cargo before it's sold.
- A potential 'money laundering' mechanic could add post-sale risk, requiring players to invest stolen cash quickly.
- The system requires skill-based hijacking and the absence of overpowered futuristic vehicles to maintain balance.
- Aims to make online sessions more intense and exciting, moving beyond GTA V's destroy-only cargo mechanics.
