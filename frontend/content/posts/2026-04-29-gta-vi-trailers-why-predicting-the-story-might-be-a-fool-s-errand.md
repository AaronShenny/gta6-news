# GTA VI Trailers: Why Predicting the Story Might Be a Fool's Errand

A recent discussion on Reddit's r/GTA6 subreddit highlights the potential unreliability of predicting Grand Theft Auto VI's story based solely on its trailers. Drawing parallels to Red Dead Redemption 2's promotional campaigns, the user points out Rockstar Games' historical tendency to include misleading elements such as character swaps, altered locations, deceptive quotes, and a mix of early and late-game content. These tactics are believed to be intentional, designed to prevent spoilers and maintain the integrity of the game's narrative until its official release, suggesting fans should temper their expectations for trailer-based story predictions.

## Key Takeaways
- Predicting GTA VI's story from its trailers is likely unreliable.
- Rockstar Games has a history of using misleading elements in their game trailers, as seen with Red Dead Redemption 2.
- Past Rockstar trailers featured character swaps, location changes for cutscenes, misleading quotes, and early missions spliced with endgame content.
- These deliberate alterations are made to avoid spoiling the game's story and mission layout.
- Some beta content shown in trailers may not appear in the final game.
- Fans should expect similar misdirection in GTA VI's promotional material.
