# GTA 6: Will the 'Busted' Mechanic Finally Evolve?

A recent Reddit discussion on r/GTA6, initiated by u/New-Guarantee-440, raises concerns about the long-standing 'busted' mechanic in the Grand Theft Auto series. The user argues that the forced surrender option, where players have 'no choice but to surrender' when confronted by police, feels unrealistic and out of place given the chaotic and often violent nature of GTA gameplay. They highlight the lack of close-quarters combat options against police, even without weapons, as a particular point of contention, citing the 'Mueller drill' as an example of realistic unarmed combat. The discussion points to games like Watch Dogs, which offer a surrender option, suggesting that GTA's current 'busted' system is an 'annoying, un-fun, unrealistic legacy feature' that GTA 6 should reconsider.

## Key Takeaways
- The 'busted' mechanic in GTA forces players to surrender without choice, regardless of the situation.
- This mechanic is perceived as unrealistic given the 'crazy shit' players engage in within the game.
- The lack of options to fight police in close quarters, even unarmed, is highlighted as unrealistic.
- Comparison is drawn to Watch Dogs, which offers a surrender option.
- The current 'busted' system is criticized as an 'annoying, un-fun, unrealistic legacy feature' that needs an update for GTA 6.

## FAQ
**What is the main criticism of the 'busted' mechanic in GTA?**
The main criticism is that it forces players to surrender without any alternative, which feels unrealistic and inconsistent with the game's overall chaotic and action-packed nature.

**Why do players find the 'busted' mechanic unrealistic?**
Players find it unrealistic because, despite engaging in high-stakes criminal activities, they are given no option but to surrender to police, even in close-quarters combat situations where a fight might be more plausible.

**Are there other games that handle surrender differently?**
Yes, the discussion specifically mentions Watch Dogs as a game that incorporates a more dynamic surrender option, suggesting GTA could learn from similar implementations.
