# GTA 6 Pricing Debate: A Call for a $60 Price Tag to Challenge Industry Norms

Amidst ongoing speculation about Grand Theft Auto VI's potential price point, a user on Reddit's r/GTA6 community has voiced a strong opinion, advocating for a surprising $60 release price. The user argues that such a move would serve as a 'middle finger to the industry,' compelling other publishers to justify their current $70 price tags for what are often perceived as lower-quality 'AAAA' games. While acknowledging that they would personally purchase the game even at $80, the core of the argument criticizes what is seen as industry 'entitlement' in pushing for higher prices, viewing it as an attempt to normalize increased costs across the board. The post references Strauss Zelnick's (Take-Two CEO) previous comments on a $70-$80 price range but expresses hope for a lower price, despite skepticism given Take-Two's pricing strategies for other IPs like 2K.

## Key Takeaways
- Reddit user advocates for GTA 6 to be priced at $60.
- The primary motivation is to challenge the gaming industry's current $70 price point for other 'AAAA' titles.
- Criticism of industry 'entitlement' to raise prices, viewing it as a way to normalize higher costs for 'garbage games'.
- Acknowledgment of Take-Two CEO Strauss Zelnick's previous comments suggesting a $70-$80 price range.
- Skepticism about a $60 price point being feasible due to Take-Two's pricing history with other franchises like 2K.
