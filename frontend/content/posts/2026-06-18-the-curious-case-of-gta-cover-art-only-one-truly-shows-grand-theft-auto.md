# The Curious Case of GTA Cover Art: Only One Truly Shows Grand Theft Auto

A recent analysis of the Grand Theft Auto series' cover art, prompted by the reveal of GTA VI's cover, has brought to light a surprising observation: only one game in the franchise explicitly features the titular crime of 'grand theft auto' on its cover. While every installment allows players to steal vehicles, the article highlights that Grand Theft Auto 2 stands alone with its cover art clearly depicting a carjacking in progress, complete with a bird's-eye view reminiscent of its in-game perspective. In stark contrast, most subsequent GTA covers, including the newly unveiled GTA VI art, are collages showcasing a variety of characters, vehicles (such as speedboats, motorcycles, and supercars), and activities available within the game world, yet conspicuously omit the act of car theft itself. Examples cited include GTA VI's diverse collage, San Andreas's depiction of a drive-by shooting (a crime *from* a car, not *of* a car), and GTA IV's ambiguous police stop. The journalist concludes by expressing a desire for Rockstar Games to prominently feature actual grand theft auto on future game covers, particularly for the anticipated GTA 7.

## Key Takeaways
- The Grand Theft Auto series, despite its name, has only one game cover that explicitly shows the titular crime.
- Grand Theft Auto 2's cover uniquely depicts a carjacking in progress, presented from a bird's-eye view that mirrors its gameplay.
- Most other GTA covers, including the recent GTA VI, are collages of characters, vehicles, and activities, but lack a clear depiction of car theft.
- Examples include GTA VI's collage of various vehicles and characters, GTA: San Andreas's drive-by shooting, and GTA IV's ambiguous police stop.
- The author hopes future GTA covers, specifically for GTA 7, will feature the act of grand theft auto more prominently.
