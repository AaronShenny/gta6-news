# GTA 6 Single-Player DLC Hopes: Will Rockstar Revisit Story Expansions?

A recent Reddit discussion initiated by u/OGAnimeGokuSolos delves into the community's expectations and hopes for single-player DLCs in the upcoming Grand Theft Auto 6. The core argument presented suggests that dedicated single-player story expansions are unlikely, primarily due to two historical factors: the reportedly disappointing sales figures of Grand Theft Auto IV's 'Episodes from Liberty City' expansions, and the overwhelming, ongoing success and profitability of GTA Online. The post speculates that if Rockstar Games and Take-Two Interactive were to release single-player DLCs for GTA 6 and they failed to meet sales expectations, the companies would likely shift their focus entirely to the game's online component, further diminishing prospects for future story-driven content.

## Key Takeaways
- Hopes for single-player DLCs in GTA 6 are low among some fans.
- Past single-player DLCs (GTA IV: Episodes from Liberty City) reportedly had disappointing sales.
- The massive success of GTA Online has shifted focus away from single-player expansions.
- If GTA 6 single-player DLCs underperform, Rockstar/Take-Two may fully prioritize the online mode.

## FAQ
**Will GTA 6 have single-player DLCs?**
The article suggests it's unlikely, citing past sales performance of GTA IV expansions and the current focus on GTA Online as key reasons.

**Why did Rockstar reportedly stop making single-player DLCs after GTA IV?**
According to the discussion, it was due to the disappointing sales of 'Episodes from Liberty City' and the subsequent success and revenue generated by GTA Online.

**What if GTA 6 single-player DLCs are released but don't sell well?**
The article speculates that Rockstar and Take-Two would then fully concentrate their efforts on the online component of GTA 6.
