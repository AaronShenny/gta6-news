# GTA 6 Preorder Buzz Drives Take-Two Stock to Significant Gains

Rockstar Games' parent company, Take-Two Interactive, saw its stock price jump by over 6% today, adding more than $2 billion to its market capitalization. This surge is attributed to credible rumors, reportedly stemming from a Best Buy leak, suggesting that preorders for the highly anticipated Grand Theft Auto 6 could commence as early as May 18. Investors appear to view this development as a strong indicator that the game's November 19 release date for PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X|S is firm and unlikely to face further delays. While Take-Two's stock had previously experienced a dip earlier this year due to concerns surrounding AI game development technology, the current market response reflects renewed confidence in GTA 6's imminent launch and its projected financial impact. Rockstar has yet to officially announce preorder details, but Take-Two confirms a major marketing push this summer.

## Key Takeaways
- Take-Two Interactive's stock price surged over 6%, increasing its market cap by more than $2 billion.
- The stock jump is linked to rumors of GTA 6 preorders potentially starting on Monday, May 18.
- Investors see the preorder news as a strong sign that GTA 6's November 19 release date will hold, avoiding further delays.
- The preorder rumor originated from a reputable Best Buy leak.
- Take-Two's stock had previously fallen by about 4% year-to-date, partly due to investor reactions to Google's AI game-creation system, Project Genie.
- Rockstar Games has not officially announced a preorder date, but Take-Two plans a marketing push for the game this summer.
- GTA 6 is set to launch on November 19 for PS5 and Xbox Series X|S.
- The game will not be available on PC at launch, as PC is not considered the "core" market.
- Preorder launch is expected to reveal the game's price, speculated to be $70, $80, or higher.
