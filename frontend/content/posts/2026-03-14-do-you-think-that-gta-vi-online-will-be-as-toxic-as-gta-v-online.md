# Do You Think That GTA VI Online Will Be As Toxic As GTA V Online?

A community discussion initiated by Reddit user /u/OffRoadAventures explores the anticipated toxicity levels in Grand Theft Auto VI Online, contrasting it with the perceived increase in aggressive player behavior observed over a decade in Grand Theft Auto V Online. The user expresses hope that an initial absence of 'over-the-top' vehicles and explosive weaponry, such as flying bikes and RC cars, might foster a more skill-based player-versus-player environment. This, in turn, could potentially deter griefers and 'bumper clickers' who often lack genuine combat skill. The sentiment highlights a desire for a more laid-back experience, where random encounters aren't immediately hostile, and questions whether the most aggressive 'tryhards' will eventually migrate to GTA VI Online or remain in the older game.

## Key Takeaways
- Player concern over the escalating toxicity and aggression in GTA V Online.
- Hope that GTA VI Online's initial stages, without 'over-the-top' vehicles and weapons, could lead to a less toxic environment.
- Speculation that a more skill-based PvP system in GTA VI Online might discourage griefing.
- Frustration with the difficulty of finding casual, non-aggressive players in GTA V Online.
- Questioning whether 'tryhard' players will transition to GTA VI Online or persist in GTA V Online.

## FAQ
**Will GTA VI Online be as toxic as GTA V Online?**
The article discusses community concerns regarding the potential for GTA VI Online to replicate or even surpass the toxicity seen in GTA V Online, particularly in its later years.

There's hope that an initial lack of 'over-the-top' vehicles and explosive weaponry could lead to a more skill-based PvP experience, potentially discouraging griefers and fostering a less aggressive community.

**Will the most aggressive 'tryhard' players migrate to GTA VI Online?**
The discussion raises the question of whether dedicated 'tryhards' will eventually move to GTA VI Online or if a segment of them might remain in GTA V Online, influencing the new game's player dynamics.
