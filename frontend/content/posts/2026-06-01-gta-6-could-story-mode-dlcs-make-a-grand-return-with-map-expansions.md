# GTA 6: Could Story Mode DLCs Make a Grand Return with Map Expansions?

A gaming journalist speculates that Grand Theft Auto 6 may mark the return of story mode DLCs, a highly requested feature absent in GTA V and Red Dead Redemption 2. This prediction is fueled by the GTA 6 Trailer 2's introduction of 'Gloriana,' a new state possibly based on Georgia, suggesting a massive, multi-state map unprecedented in the franchise. The author posits that Rockstar could expand the game's world and release story-driven content set in these new areas, potentially alongside online expansions, offering a more ambitious post-launch experience than previous titles.

## Key Takeaways
- The prediction is a journalist's idea, not a leak or rumor.
- GTA 6 Trailer 2 introduced 'Gloriana,' a new state possibly based on Georgia, suggesting a multi-state map.
- The author believes Rockstar might bring back story mode DLCs, addressing a common criticism of GTA 5 and RDR2.
- These potential story DLCs could be integrated with map and online expansions, featuring new content set in Gloriana and other states.
- This approach would differ from GTA 4's standalone story DLCs by combining them with broader game world growth.
