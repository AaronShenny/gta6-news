# GTA 6 Publisher Take-Two Reveals Major Past Financial Losses, Unlikely to Revive MLB 2K

Take-Two Interactive, the powerhouse publisher preparing for the highly anticipated GTA 6, has candidly addressed past financial struggles within its diverse portfolio. CEO Strauss Zelnick confirmed in an interview that the company suffered 'a great deal of money' with its MLB 2K baseball series, making a return to the MLB-licensed video game market highly improbable. Zelnick cited the market's 'too small' size and the franchise's poor critical reception, particularly MLB 2K13, which was panned for its lack of innovation. The MLB 2K series ran for approximately a decade, ending in 2014. While baseball is off the table, Take-Two continues to thrive with successful sports titles like NBA 2K, PGA Tour 2K, WWE 2K, and the recently revived TopSpin 2K tennis series. The article also touched upon Take-Two's non-simulation NFL games deal, which primarily resulted in a mobile title, NFL 2K Playmakers, as creative visions didn't fully materialize. Zelnick expressed openness to future NFL collaborations if suitable opportunities arise. Other brief mentions included updates on BioShock 4 and Borderlands 4's delayed Switch 2 edition, with the article concluding by reiterating that Take-Two's biggest upcoming title, GTA 6, is slated for release on November 19.

## Key Takeaways
- Take-Two, publisher of GTA 6, lost 'a great deal of money' on its MLB 2K baseball series.
- CEO Strauss Zelnick stated the MLB market is 'too small,' making a return to MLB-licensed games unlikely.
- The MLB 2K franchise ran from 2005 to 2014 and concluded on a weak note, with MLB 2K13 critically panned.
- Take-Two's NFL deal for non-simulation games didn't fully meet creative expectations, primarily yielding a mobile title, NFL 2K Playmakers.
- Successful sports franchises for Take-Two include NBA 2K, PGA Tour 2K, WWE 2K, and the recently revived TopSpin 2K.
- GTA 6 is highlighted as Take-Two's biggest upcoming game, with a release date of November 19.
