# GTA 6's Unintended Challenge: Real-World Locations Demolished During Its Long Development

Rockstar Games' highly anticipated Grand Theft Auto VI, slated for a 2026 release, faces an unusual hurdle: several real-world structures meticulously scouted for the game between 2015 and 2018 no longer exist. This lengthy development timeline means that while Rockstar was crafting its vibrant open world, the actual landscape it drew inspiration from continued to evolve. Notable examples include the Economy Inn in Lawtey, Florida, which was destroyed by fire and subsequently demolished in 2021. Another Miami Beach property, identified as 205 E San Marino Drive, was razed in 2022 to make way for new construction. Furthermore, the prominent Sebring Water Tower in Florida, a landmark on U.S. 27, was scheduled for demolition in May 2025 after being deemed unnecessary and too costly to maintain. These instances underscore the dynamic nature of real-world environments and the unique complexities involved in developing a game over such an extended period, potentially requiring developers to adapt their virtual world to reflect these changes or preserve a moment in time.

## Key Takeaways
- GTA 6's development included extensive real-world scouting between 2015-2018, for a game releasing in 2026.
- The Economy Inn in Lawtey, FL, a scouted location, was destroyed by fire in May 2021 and subsequently demolished.
- A residential property at 205 E San Marino Dr, Miami Beach, FL, was demolished in July 2022 to make way for new development.
- The Sebring Water Tower in Sebring, FL, a former landmark, was scheduled for demolition in May 2025 due to disuse and high maintenance costs.
