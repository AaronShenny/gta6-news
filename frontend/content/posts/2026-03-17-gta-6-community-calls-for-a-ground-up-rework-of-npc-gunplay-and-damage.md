# GTA 6: Community Calls for a Ground-Up Rework of NPC Gunplay and Damage

A recent discussion on Reddit's r/GTA6 subreddit has ignited debate over the fundamental gunplay mechanics in Rockstar's previous titles, Grand Theft Auto V and Red Dead Redemption 2, advocating for significant adjustments in the upcoming Grand Theft Auto 6. The core argument centers on what many perceive as an unbalanced system where NPCs exhibit unrealistically high accuracy from any distance and situation, while player damage taken feels underwhelming due to readily available health regeneration and healing items. The author suggests that this design choice, seemingly to compensate for low damage output, ultimately detracts from the gunfight experience. The proposed solution for GTA 6 is to decrease enemy accuracy while simultaneously increasing the damage they inflict, aiming to create more tense, engaging, and tactical combat scenarios where player movement and cover strategy become more crucial.

## Key Takeaways
- NPCs in GTA 5 and RDR2 exhibit unrealistically high accuracy, hitting players from any distance or situation.
- Player damage taken is often 'underwhelming' due to infinite regeneration (GTA 5) and an abundance of healing items (RDR2).
- This balance is believed to make gunfights less tense and engaging.
- The proposed solution for GTA 6 is to lower NPC accuracy while increasing their damage output.
- These changes aim to foster more tactical, tense, and interesting gunfights where player movement and cover are more impactful.

## FAQ
**What is the primary criticism of gunfights in GTA 5 and RDR2?**
The main criticism is that NPCs possess unrealistically high accuracy regardless of distance or situation, while the damage players take is often underwhelming due to easy health regeneration and abundant healing items.

**What solution is proposed for GTA 6's gunplay?**
The community suggests lowering NPC accuracy while simultaneously increasing the damage they deal to players.

**How would these changes improve GTA 6 gunfights?**
These adjustments are expected to make gunfights more tense, tactical, and engaging, allowing players to utilize movement and cover more effectively to avoid incoming fire.
