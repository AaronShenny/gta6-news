# GTA 6 Delays Point to Robust Single-Player Experience, Not Online Focus, Argues Fan

A recent discussion on Reddit's r/GTA6 community suggests that the ongoing development delays for Grand Theft Auto VI are a strong indicator of Rockstar Games' commitment to delivering a comprehensive and high-quality single-player experience. Contrary to popular belief that Rockstar might be deferring single-player content in favor of its highly successful online counterpart, the user argues that such delays would be unlikely if an online-first strategy were truly in play. The post posits that GTA VI's story mode could be exceptionally robust, potentially even exceeding the acclaimed narrative depth of Red Dead Redemption 2, with the online mode likely launching at a later date.

## Key Takeaways
- GTA 6's development delays are interpreted as a positive sign for its single-player content.
- The author refutes the idea that Rockstar Games is solely prioritizing GTA Online over story mode.
- Belief that GTA VI's single-player story will be robust and substantial.
- The story mode is expected to be potentially better than Red Dead Redemption 2's narrative.
- GTA VI's online component is anticipated to be released later than the single-player story.
