# GTA 6 Fan Concept Imagines a More Dynamic Wanted System: What If NPCs Had to Call the Police?

A recent discussion among Grand Theft Auto fans has sparked interest in a potential overhaul of the series' iconic wanted system for GTA 6. Currently, committing a crime instantly alerts the police, leading to an immediate wanted level. However, a compelling fan concept suggests a more dynamic approach: what if NPCs in GTA 6 had to physically call the police via their smartphones after witnessing a crime? This change could allow players to escape before a report is made, strategically silence witnesses, or face escalated responses based on the number of calls, injecting a new layer of realism and tactical depth into criminal encounters.

## Key Takeaways
- Current GTA games feature an instantaneous wanted system where police 'just know' about crimes.
- The proposed GTA 6 concept suggests NPCs would actively use smartphones to call the police after witnessing a crime.
- This system would allow players to potentially avoid a wanted level by escaping quickly before a call is made.
- The intensity and speed of police response could be influenced by the number of NPCs making calls.
- Players could interact with NPCs to prevent calls, such as intimidating or stopping them.
- The concept aims to make criminal actions feel more dynamic and realistic, aligning with modern smartphone ubiquity.
- Discussion includes whether such a system would enhance gameplay or become an annoying mechanic.

## FAQ
**What is the core idea behind this GTA 6 wanted system concept?**
The core idea is that instead of an automatic wanted level, NPCs would have to manually call the police using their in-game smartphones after witnessing a crime, making the system more dynamic and realistic.

**How would this new system change gameplay in GTA 6?**
It would introduce new tactical elements, allowing players to escape before a call is made, intervene to stop NPCs from calling, or face varying police responses based on how many witnesses report the crime.

**Would this system make crimes more challenging or more rewarding?**
It aims to make crimes feel more dynamic and strategic. While it could add a layer of challenge by requiring players to manage witnesses, it could also be more rewarding by allowing players to evade justice through clever actions.

**Has Rockstar Games indicated any plans for such a wanted system?**
No, this is purely a fan-generated concept from the community and there has been no official word from Rockstar Games regarding changes to the wanted system in GTA 6.
