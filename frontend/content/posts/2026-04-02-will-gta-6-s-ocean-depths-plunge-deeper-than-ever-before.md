# Will GTA 6's Ocean Depths Plunge Deeper Than Ever Before?

The community is buzzing with anticipation regarding the underwater environments in Grand Theft Auto 6, specifically questioning how deep players will be able to explore. Drawing a stark contrast with Grand Theft Auto V, where the ocean floor and submersible limits were notably shallow (around 450 feet), many believe this was a technical limitation of the PlayStation 3 era. With GTA 6 set in a Florida-inspired Vice City, players are hopeful for a more realistic and expansive aquatic experience. Real-world Florida's continental shelf extends for hundreds of nautical miles, eventually reaching depths of approximately 800 meters at its edge. This has fueled speculation that GTA 6 could offer a significantly deeper ocean, potentially incorporating the bathypelagic zone and even hiding deep-sea Easter eggs, such as a giant squid.

## Key Takeaways
- GTA V's ocean was notably shallow, with a maximum depth of about 450 feet for submersibles and the seafloor.
- The shallow depth in GTA V is largely attributed to technical limitations of the PS3 era.
- Real-world Florida features an extensive continental shelf, reaching depths of roughly 800 meters at its outer edge.
- Fans are speculating and hoping that GTA 6 will offer significantly deeper oceans, potentially mirroring real-world depths.
- There is anticipation for unique deep-sea content and Easter eggs, such as giant squid encounters, in GTA 6's underwater world.
