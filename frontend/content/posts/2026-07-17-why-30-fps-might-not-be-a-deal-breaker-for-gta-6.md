# Why 30 FPS Might Not Be a Deal-Breaker for GTA 6

In a recent Reddit discussion titled 'Why 60fps won't matter,' user u/Ok-End5231 argues that a stable 30 frames per second (FPS) for the upcoming Grand Theft Auto 6 might not be the catastrophe many gamers fear. The post challenges the common belief that all modern games *must* run at 60 FPS to be enjoyable, pointing to critically acclaimed story-driven titles like L.A. Noire that thrived on immersion, atmosphere, detailed facial animations, and compelling storytelling, rather than lightning-fast reaction times. The author suggests that GTA has historically prioritized living within its expansive world over being a competitive shooter. Therefore, if Rockstar Games can deliver a locked 30 FPS experience with exceptional visuals, intricate physics, advanced AI, and a deep world simulation, this would be preferable to an inconsistent frame rate fluctuating between 45-60 FPS. While acknowledging that 60 FPS would be ideal if achievable, the core argument emphasizes prioritizing consistency and the overall immersive experience over chasing a higher frame rate at the expense of other crucial elements.

## Key Takeaways
- A stable 30 FPS might not ruin GTA 6, contrary to popular belief.
- Many great story-driven games are enjoyable and immersive at 30 FPS (e.g., L.A. Noire).
- GTA's core appeal lies in world immersion and storytelling, not competitive speed.
- Prioritizing consistent 30 FPS with superior visuals, physics, AI, and world simulation is better than an unstable higher frame rate.
- While 60 FPS is desirable, overall experience and stability should take precedence.

## FAQ
**Is 60 FPS essential for all games?**
The article argues against this, suggesting that for story-driven, immersive games like GTA, a stable 30 FPS can still provide an excellent experience.

**What aspects should GTA 6 prioritize over frame rate?**
The author suggests prioritizing consistent frame rate, amazing visuals, physics, AI, and world simulation for a truly immersive experience.

**Does a lower frame rate necessarily mean a worse game?**
Not according to the article, which cites games like L.A. Noire as examples of titles that were highly enjoyable and immersive at 30 FPS due to their focus on storytelling and atmosphere.

**What if GTA 6 runs at an unstable higher FPS?**
The author explicitly states they would prefer a locked 30 FPS over an unstable 45-60 FPS that constantly fluctuates, as consistency enhances the experience.
