# Will GTA 6's Hunting Mechanics Rival Red Dead Redemption 2's Depth?

A recent discussion has emerged regarding the potential sophistication of hunting mechanics in Grand Theft Auto 6, drawing keen comparisons to previous Rockstar titles. In GTA 5, hunting was a basic activity exclusive to Trevor, offering no options to sell or butcher animal parts. This stands in stark contrast to Red Dead Redemption 2, which featured highly acclaimed, realistic, and often brutal hunting mechanics, complete with detailed animal reactions, specific skinning animations for various creatures, and a robust in-game economy for selling animal products. The author speculates that GTA 6 might adopt RDR2's intricate system, pointing to a leaked screenshot that depicts "swamp folk hauling gators" as potential evidence for significant animal interaction and an associated trade system. The discussion also considers how the two main protagonists, Jason and Lucia, might interact with such a mechanic; Jason, with his speculated prior military background, might realistically possess the skills for skinning, while Lucia, described as a "city girl," might not. The article ultimately poses a question to the community: will GTA 6 implement a deep, RDR2-style hunting system, allow players to simply kill and sell carcasses, or revert to GTA 5's more limited, sport-only approach?
