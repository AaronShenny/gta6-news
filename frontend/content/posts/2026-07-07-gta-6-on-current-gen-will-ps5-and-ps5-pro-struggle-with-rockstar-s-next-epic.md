# GTA 6 on Current-Gen: Will PS5 and PS5 Pro Struggle with Rockstar's Next Epic?

A recent Reddit post has ignited a discussion among gamers regarding the anticipated performance of Grand Theft Auto 6 on current-generation consoles, specifically the base PlayStation 5 and PS5 Pro. The original poster expresses personal skepticism, noting that even their PS5 Pro occasionally struggles with modern titles, raising concerns about the base PS5's capability.

The user draws parallels to historical performance issues with Rockstar's highly optimized games, recalling significant lag in dense urban areas like Saint Denis when Red Dead Redemption 2 was played on the base PS4. They also mention that older titles such as GTA 5 fared even worse at launch. Despite these past experiences and current-gen console limitations, many players reportedly still plan to experience GTA 6 on the original PS5.

This observation prompts questions from the author: Is this choice driven by financial constraints, or do players simply prioritize playing the game immediately over achieving optimal performance and visual quality? The post also explores the possibility of players delaying their first playthrough, waiting 1.5-2 years for a more robust, optimized version, akin to how some approached RDR2, though acknowledging that such patience is rare.

## Key Takeaways
- Concerns are raised about GTA 6's potential performance on current-gen consoles, including the base PS5 and PS5 Pro.
- Past Rockstar titles like Red Dead Redemption 2 (on PS4) and GTA 5 experienced significant performance issues at launch, particularly in dense environments.
- The discussion questions players' motivations for planning to play GTA 6 on base PS5 despite potential performance and quality compromises.
- Some players might consider waiting for a more optimized, later version of the game, similar to how a few approached RDR2's initial release.
