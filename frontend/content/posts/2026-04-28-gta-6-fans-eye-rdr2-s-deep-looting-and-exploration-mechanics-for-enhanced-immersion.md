# GTA 6 Fans Eye RDR2's Deep Looting and Exploration Mechanics for Enhanced Immersion

Inspired by Red Dead Redemption 2's intricate world design, a dedicated fan has voiced their desires for Grand Theft Auto VI, particularly focusing on an expanded looting and exploration system. The user highlighted RDR2's ability to thoroughly loot items from houses, store them, and then sell or craft, envisioning a similar mechanic for GTA 6. This would allow players to discover valuable items like jewelry within various buildings, offering options to wear them or sell for cash, thereby making the vast open world significantly more rewarding to explore. While acknowledging that advanced features such as 'smart' NPCs with routines and the 'greet and antagonize' system are likely already planned, the core request remains for more accessible interior spaces and a robust item looting and storage system to deepen player immersion.

## Key Takeaways
- A Reddit user (u/ajoota98) proposes incorporating RDR2's detailed looting system into GTA 6.
- The suggested feature would allow players to explore buildings, find items (e.g., jewelry), and then sell or use them.
- This mechanic is seen as a way to make exploration more rewarding and provide new ways to hide collectibles.
- The user also anticipates 'smart' NPCs with routines and the 'greet and antagonize' system from RDR2 will be present in GTA 6.
- The primary desire is for more enterable buildings and a robust item looting/storage system to enhance player immersion.

## FAQ
**What RDR2 feature is the user most keen on for GTA 6?**
The user is most keen on RDR2's detailed looting system, which allows players to explore buildings, find items, store them, and then sell or use them for crafting.

**How would this feature enhance GTA 6's gameplay?**
It would make exploration significantly more rewarding, provide additional ways to hide collectibles across the map, and deepen player immersion by offering more interactive interior spaces.

**Are other RDR2 features expected to be in GTA 6?**
Yes, the user believes that 'smart' NPCs with their own routines and the 'greet and antagonize' system are highly likely to be included in GTA 6.

**Who originally submitted this idea?**
The idea was originally submitted by Reddit user /u/ajoota98.
