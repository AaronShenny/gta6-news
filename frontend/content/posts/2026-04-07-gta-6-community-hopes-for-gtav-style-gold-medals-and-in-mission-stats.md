# GTA 6: Community Hopes for GTAV-Style Gold Medals and In-Mission Stats

The Grand Theft Auto community is already looking ahead to GTA 6's gameplay mechanics, with a recent Reddit post sparking debate over mission replayability and completion systems. The sentiment, shared by u/IlxSeverzzxlI, strongly favors Grand Theft Auto V's gold medal system over Red Dead Redemption 2's more stringent requirements. Players appreciate GTAV's flexibility, which allows objectives for gold medals to be completed across multiple attempts, contrasting sharply with RDR2's demand for all objectives in a single run. Furthermore, the discussion calls for the inclusion of real-time mission timers and accuracy percentages within GTA 6's gameplay, particularly during mission replays, to enhance the challenge and player feedback experience.

## Key Takeaways
- Players prefer GTA V's gold medal system for its flexibility, allowing objectives to be completed across multiple mission attempts.
- Red Dead Redemption 2's requirement to achieve all gold medal objectives in a single run is seen as less player-friendly.
- A strong desire exists for GTA 6 to incorporate in-mission timers and accuracy percentages.
- These real-time performance metrics are particularly requested for mission replays to aid in mastery and improve replayability.

## FAQ
**What's the main difference between GTAV and RDR2's gold medal systems?**
GTAV allows players to achieve gold medal objectives across multiple attempts of a mission, while RDR2 typically requires all objectives to be met in a single playthrough to earn a gold medal.

**Why are players asking for in-mission timers and accuracy percentages in GTA 6?**
Players believe that displaying real-time timers and accuracy percentages, especially during mission replays, would provide better feedback, enhance the challenge, and assist them in perfecting their performance to achieve gold medals.
