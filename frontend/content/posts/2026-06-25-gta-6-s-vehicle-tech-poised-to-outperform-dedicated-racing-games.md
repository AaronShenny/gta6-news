# GTA 6's Vehicle Tech Poised to Outperform Dedicated Racing Games

According to recent insights, Rockstar Games is making a significant leap in vehicle fidelity for Grand Theft Auto 6. The upcoming title will reportedly allocate substantial polycount budgets to true 3D geometry, moving away from 2D texture tricks. This includes real 3D panel gaps and meticulously modeled, era-specific suspension components like wishbones and control arms for the underbody. Furthermore, GTA 6 is set to be the first in the series to adopt Physically Based Rendering (PBR), a significant upgrade from GTA V’s older phong shading. This advanced approach to vehicle design and rendering suggests that GTA 6’s in-game vehicles could visually and technically surpass many dedicated racing games on the market.

## Key Takeaways
- Significant shift to true 3D geometry for vehicle models.
- Introduction of real 3D panel gaps, replacing flat 2D seams with painted-on ambient occlusion.
- Detailed, era-specific 3D suspension components (e.g., wishbones, control arms) modeled for lower chassis and underbody.
- First GTA game to implement Physically Based Rendering (PBR), moving beyond GTA V's phong shading.
- GTA 6's vehicle technology is anticipated to potentially outperform dedicated racing games.
