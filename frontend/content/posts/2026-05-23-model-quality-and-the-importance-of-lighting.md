# Model quality and the importance of lighting

Recent speculation surrounding the second GTA 6 trailer suggested that Lucia's character model in a specific scene appeared less detailed, leading to rumors of an older build or gameplay footage. However, this article clarifies that the perceived difference in model quality is primarily due to suboptimal lighting in that particular scene, rather than a change in the model itself. The author draws a parallel to a similar situation with Arthur's character model in an RDR2 trailer, where lighting conditions significantly altered his perceived detail. The core argument is that Rockstar does not arbitrarily switch model quality within a single trailer; instead, effective lighting is crucial for showcasing character models and environments, with the author anticipating stunning visuals, especially sunsets, in GTA 6.

## Key Takeaways
- Rumors suggested varying model quality for Lucia in a specific GTA 6 Trailer 2 scene.
- The perceived lack of detail in Lucia's model was attributed to poor lighting in that scene, not an older build or gameplay footage.
- A similar situation occurred with Arthur Morgan's character model in an RDR2 trailer, where lighting significantly impacted perceived detail.
- Rockstar does not switch character model quality within the same trailer.
- Lighting is a critical factor in how character models and overall scenes are perceived.
- The author anticipates "drop-dead gorgeous" visuals, particularly sunsets, in GTA 6, thanks to Rockstar's advanced tech.
