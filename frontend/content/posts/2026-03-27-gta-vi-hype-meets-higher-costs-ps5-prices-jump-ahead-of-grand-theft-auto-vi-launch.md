# GTA VI Hype Meets Higher Costs: PS5 Prices Jump Ahead of Grand Theft Auto VI Launch

For many avid gamers, the impending release of Grand Theft Auto VI serves as the ultimate catalyst for finally investing in a PlayStation 5. However, that long-awaited upgrade just became significantly more expensive. Sony has confirmed a global price increase for its PS5 consoles and the PlayStation Portal, with these new figures taking effect on April 2, 2026. This means that instead of enjoying potential late-generation deals, prospective GTA VI players will now face higher retail prices across all major markets, including the U.S., U.K., Europe, and Japan. Sony attributes these adjustments to prevailing global economic pressures.

## Key Takeaways
- Sony has implemented a global price increase for PlayStation 5 consoles and the PlayStation Portal.
- The new pricing structure will be effective starting April 2, 2026.
- This move significantly impacts gamers who were planning to purchase a PS5 specifically in anticipation of Grand Theft Auto VI's release.
- New prices include: U.S. PS5 at $649.99, Digital at $599.99, Pro at $899.99; U.K. PS5 at £569.99, Digital at £519.99, Pro at £789.99; Europe PS5 at €649.99, Digital at €599.99, Pro at €899.99; Japan PS5 at ¥97,980, Digital at ¥89,980, Pro at ¥137,980. PlayStation Portal also sees increases.
- Sony cites broader global economic pressure as the reason for these price adjustments.

## FAQ
**When do the new PS5 prices take effect?**
The new, increased prices for PlayStation 5 consoles and PlayStation Portal will be effective starting April 2, 2026.

**Why did Sony raise the prices of PS5 consoles?**
Sony has stated that the price hikes are a response to broader global economic pressures.

**Which PS5 models are affected by the price increase?**
All PlayStation 5 models, including the standard PS5, PS5 Digital Edition, PS5 Pro, and the PlayStation Portal, are subject to the price adjustments.

**How much does a standard PS5 now cost in the U.S.?**
In the U.S., the standard PS5 is now priced at $649.99, while the PS5 Digital Edition is $599.99.
