## FAQ
**What does 'internet activation' mean for a physical game?**
It typically means that even after installing a game from a physical disc, players must connect to the internet to validate their copy with the publisher's servers before they can play, often even for single-player content.

**Why is mandatory internet activation a concern for gamers?**
Concerns often include issues with true game ownership, the inability to play offline, limitations on reselling used games, potential server shutdowns rendering games unplayable in the future, and general distrust of aggressive DRM.

**Are there precedents for physical games requiring online activation?**
Yes, many PC games have historically required online activation. Some console games also implement similar measures, particularly for accessing day-one patches or certain game modes, though mandatory activation for the base physical game is more contentious.

**What are the arguments for publishers implementing such a system?**
Publishers often argue that mandatory internet activation helps combat piracy, ensures legitimate copies are being played, facilitates game updates, and allows for better tracking of player engagement and data.
