# Decoding Grand Theft Auto VI's Development Timeline: A Fan's Speculative Journey

A Reddit user has put forth a highly speculative, yet meticulously detailed, timeline for Grand Theft Auto VI's development. The core premise suggests that Rockstar Games typically initiates work on a new GTA title approximately two years after the previous installment's release. Based on GTA V's 2013 launch, the author posits that the initial conceptual phase for GTA VI commenced around 2016, with a definitive decision to set the game in a modern-day Vice City by that same year. The theory breaks down the subsequent years into distinct development stages, including design, world-building, performance capture, and technological advancement. Employing a recurring 'plus three years' pattern, the author projects a Grand Theft Auto VI release in 2026, followed by a PC version in 2028, and even extends predictions to the future of GTA VII.

## Key Takeaways
- Rockstar Games is theorized to begin development on new GTA titles roughly two years after the preceding game's release.
- Based on GTA V's 2013 launch, GTA VI's conceptualization is believed to have started around 2016.

- The speculative development phases include: Concept (2016-2017), Designing (2017-2018), World Building (2018-2019), Performance Capture (2019-2020), and Core Technology/Development (2020-2022).
- A 'plus three years' pattern is used to predict major milestones:

-     2025: Trailer 2 is predicted to release.

-     2028: Grand Theft Auto VI PC version is speculated to launch.
- The theory extends to GTA VII, predicting its start in 2031 and release in 2037, potentially set in a future London (2029).
