# GTA 6's World Events: A Shift Towards Observation Over Interaction

According to a Reddit user's theory, Grand Theft Auto 6 is poised to revolutionize how world events function, moving significantly beyond the binary, action-focused encounters of previous GTA titles. While Red Dead Redemption 2 introduced dialogue options, GTA 6 is anticipated to feature numerous scenarios where players act as observers rather than direct participants. Citing an instance from Trailer 2 depicting urban crime and abuse of authority without protagonist Jason's intervention, the author suggests events will lack clear good/evil narratives, unlike GTA 5. This shift implies a world less centered on player agency and more on the player being a small, sometimes passive, part of a larger, evolving environment, where interference in certain situations would feel immersion-breaking.

## Key Takeaways
- GTA 6's world events are expected to differ vastly from past titles.
- Previous GTA games featured binary, action-based random events.
- Red Dead Redemption 2 evolved this with dialogue options, but GTA 6 may go further.
- The author predicts many GTA 6 events will require player observation rather than direct interaction.
- A scene from GTA 6 Trailer 2, showing urban crime without player intervention, is cited as an example.
- Events are likely to lack clear good/evil narratives, unlike simpler scenarios in GTA 5.
- The game world is envisioned as existing independently, with the player being a small part of it.
- Interfering in certain organic world events would potentially break immersion for the player.
