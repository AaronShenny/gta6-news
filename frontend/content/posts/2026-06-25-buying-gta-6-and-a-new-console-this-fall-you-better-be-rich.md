# Buying GTA 6 And A New Console This Fall? You Better Be Rich

As Grand Theft Auto 6 prepares for its highly anticipated launch on November 19, 2026, exclusively on PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X|S, prospective players face an alarming financial hurdle. Unlike previous GTA releases where console prices had significantly declined, the current-gen consoles are projected to be substantially more expensive than their 2020 launch prices. This inflation is largely attributed to the AI industry's insatiable demand for computer chips, driving up hardware costs across the board. The standard PS5 and Xbox Series X, originally $500, are now expected to be $650 and $800 respectively, with the Xbox Series S also climbing to $500. This generation has notably lacked cheaper, redesigned console models. Factoring in the game's cost—up to $100 for the Ultimate Edition—a new player could be looking at an investment of $900 or more just to jump into the world of GTA 6. The article warns that this trend could transform video gaming from an accessible pastime into a luxury hobby, a stark departure from the accessibility that underpinned the massive success of past Rockstar titles.

## Key Takeaways
- Grand Theft Auto 6 launches on November 19, 2026, exclusively for PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X|S.
- Unlike past GTA launches, current-gen console prices are projected to be significantly higher in 2026 than their 2020 launch prices.
- The primary driver for increased console prices is the AI industry's escalating demand for computer chips.
- Standard PS5 and Xbox Series X, originally $500, are now expected to cost $650 and $800 respectively, with the Xbox Series S also rising to $500.
- This console generation has not seen the typical price reductions or cheaper redesigns observed in previous cycles.
- The total cost for a new console and the Ultimate Edition of GTA 6 could exceed $900, creating an unprecedented financial barrier for a Rockstar game.
- The article expresses concern that this trend could shift gaming into a luxury hobby, limiting its accessibility to a broader audience.
