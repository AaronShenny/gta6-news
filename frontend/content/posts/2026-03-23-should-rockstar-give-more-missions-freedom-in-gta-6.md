# Should Rockstar give more missions freedom in GTA 6?

A recent discussion on Reddit's r/GTA6 community delves into whether Rockstar Games should grant players more freedom during story missions in the upcoming Grand Theft Auto 6. The original poster argues against increased mission freedom for narrative segments, positing that Rockstar's current model—offering a vast open world for free exploration while maintaining a more linear, controlled experience for story missions—is a fair and effective design choice. While acknowledging that flexible mission design is possible, as demonstrated by content like Cayo Perico, the author believes Rockstar's decision to keep story missions structured is crucial for preserving a well-crafted narrative experience.

## Key Takeaways
- The core debate centers on introducing more player freedom into GTA 6's story missions.
- The author advocates for Rockstar's established design: a free-roaming open world coupled with rule-based story missions.
- This approach is seen as a 'fair trade,' integrating a linear story experience within an open-world environment.
- Increased mission freedom is believed to potentially undermine the intended experience of a carefully crafted story.
- Rockstar's Cayo Perico heist demonstrates their capacity for flexible mission design, but they often opt for structure in story missions for valid reasons.

## FAQ
**What is the core debate regarding GTA 6 missions?**
The core debate is whether Rockstar should introduce more player freedom and less linearity into the story missions of Grand Theft Auto 6.

**Why does the author support Rockstar's traditional approach to story missions?**
The author believes Rockstar's model of providing a free-roaming open world alongside structured story missions is a fair compromise, ensuring a well-crafted narrative experience isn't diluted by excessive player freedom.

**Has Rockstar shown the ability to create flexible missions?**
Yes, the Cayo Perico heist in GTA Online is cited as an example of Rockstar's capability for flexible mission design, though they typically reserve this for non-story content.
