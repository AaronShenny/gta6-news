# GTA 6: Could Vehicles Become as Vital as RDR2's Horses?

Inspired by the deep connection players formed with horses in Red Dead Redemption 2, a journalist suggests Grand Theft Auto 6 could elevate vehicles from disposable assets to essential, immersive components. The article proposes integrating systems like vehicle maintenance (refueling, cleaning, repairs), a 'familiarity' mechanic that improves vehicle performance with use, and a modern equivalent of RDR2's 'whistling' to summon vehicles via texting or calling an NPC. Crucially, it advocates for a limited character inventory, making vehicle trunks the primary storage for weapons and gear, aiming to enhance realism and create more cinematic gameplay moments.

## Key Takeaways
- Advocates for GTA 6 vehicles to have the same importance as horses in RDR2.
- Proposes vehicle maintenance (refueling, cleaning, repairs) to replace biological care.
- Suggests a 'vehicle familiarity' system where performance improves with use (e.g., faster starts, better handling).
- Recommends a modern 'whistling' equivalent, allowing players to text/call an NPC to bring their vehicle.
- Pushes for limited character inventory, making vehicle trunks the primary storage for weapons and gear.
- Believes these changes would significantly boost immersion and create more grounded, cinematic gameplay experiences.

## FAQ
**What is the core idea for vehicles in GTA 6?**
The core idea is to make vehicles as important and immersive as horses were in Red Dead Redemption 2, moving away from the disposable vehicle model of GTA 5.

**How would vehicle maintenance work in GTA 6?**
Players would need to refuel at gas stations, clean dirt, mud, or blood off their vehicles, and repair damage like broken windshields or lights.

**What would replace the 'bonding' system from RDR2?**
A 'vehicle familiarity' system, where a car's performance (e.g., engine starts, handling) improves the more you use it.

**How would a limited inventory system affect gameplay?**
Players would primarily store weapons in their car's trunk, needing to retrieve them for missions, which is suggested to enhance realism and cinematic moments.
