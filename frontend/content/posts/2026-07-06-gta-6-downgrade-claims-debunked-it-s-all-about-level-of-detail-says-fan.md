# GTA 6 'Downgrade' Claims Debunked: It's All About Level of Detail, Says Fan

A recent post on the r/GTA6 subreddit humorously yet effectively debunks the growing sentiment among some fans that Grand Theft Auto VI has already seen a graphical 'downgrade' between scenes in its second trailer. The original poster, u/Sdejo, sarcastically titled their post, "Just found out R* downgraded GTA VI in Trailer 2 from one scene to another," before clarifying their intent. The user explains that what some perceive as a downgrade is simply a misunderstanding of how game graphics, specifically Levels of Detail (LoD), work. They highlight that comparing different LoD levels, which are dynamically rendered based on distance and importance, can lead to false conclusions about a game's overall graphical fidelity. The post includes a second image as 'proof,' pointing to differences in tire details as an example of varying LoD, not a permanent downgrade.

## FAQ
**What is the main argument against the GTA 6 downgrade claims?**
The main argument is that perceived graphical differences are due to varying Levels of Detail (LoD) rather than an actual downgrade. Games dynamically adjust detail based on distance and importance to optimize performance.

**What is Level of Detail (LoD) in gaming?**
Level of Detail (LoD) is a technique used in computer graphics to reduce the complexity of a 3D model or object as it moves further away from the viewer or becomes less important, thereby improving rendering performance without a noticeable loss of quality.

**How did the Reddit user prove their point?**
The user provided a second image, likely a comparison, pointing out differences in details like car tires as an example of different LoD levels being rendered, rather than a permanent graphical downgrade.

**Is it common for games to use different LoD levels in trailers?**
Yes, it is standard practice for game engines to utilize LoD systems. Even within a trailer, different scenes or objects at varying distances will naturally display different levels of detail.
