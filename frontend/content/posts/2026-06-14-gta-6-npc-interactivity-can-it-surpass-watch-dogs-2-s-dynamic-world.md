# GTA 6 NPC Interactivity: Can it Surpass Watch Dogs 2's Dynamic World?

A recent discussion on the r/GTA6 subreddit has sparked debate regarding the potential interactivity of Grand Theft Auto 6's non-player characters. User /u/PooPoox posited that Watch Dogs 2 currently holds the crown for the most dynamic NPC interactions in gaming, even surpassing the acclaimed Red Dead Redemption 2. The user attributes Watch Dogs 2's superior interactivity to its unique mechanics, such as the ability to hack and emote near NPCs, leading to diverse reactions, and even orchestrating conflicts by calling authorities on gangs. In contrast, the post suggests that while Rockstar's NPCs are well-crafted, they often lack the same breadth of 'fun ways to interact' beyond simple dialogue exchanges, raising the question of whether GTA 6 will elevate the standard for open-world NPC engagement.

## Key Takeaways
- The discussion centers on the potential for GTA 6's NPCs to be more interactive than those in Watch Dogs 2.
- Watch Dogs 2 is highlighted as having the most interactive NPCs, even more so than Red Dead Redemption 2.
- Key interactive features in Watch Dogs 2 include hacking, emoting near NPCs for varied reactions, and calling police on gangs.
- Rockstar's NPCs are noted as good but are perceived to lack diverse 'fun ways to interact' beyond basic banter.
