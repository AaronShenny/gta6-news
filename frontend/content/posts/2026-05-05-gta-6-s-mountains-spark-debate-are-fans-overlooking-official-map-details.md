# GTA 6's Mountains Spark Debate: Are Fans Overlooking Official Map Details?

A persistent misconception among Grand Theft Auto 6 enthusiasts has come to light: the existence of mountains within the game's setting of Leonida. Despite Rockstar Games officially confirming 'Mount Kalaga' via 'Visit Leonida' postcards on their website back in May, many social media users continue to express disbelief, citing Florida's famously flat topography. This has ignited a community discussion regarding whether such geographical discrepancies are immersion-breaking or simply an accepted creative liberty within a video game.

## Key Takeaways
- Many GTA 6 fans are unaware that the game's map includes mountains.
- Mount Kalaga was officially confirmed by Rockstar Games in May through 'Visit Leonida' postcards.
- Disbelief stems from Leonida's inspiration by real-life Florida, which is notably flat.
- The community is debating if the inclusion of mountains is immersion-breaking or acceptable for a video game.

## FAQ
**Are there mountains in GTA 6?**
Yes, 'Mount Kalaga' has been officially confirmed by Rockstar Games as part of the Leonida map.

**Why are some players surprised by mountains in GTA 6?**
Leonida, the game's setting, is heavily inspired by Florida, which is known for its flat landscape, leading to a perception that mountains would be an unrealistic addition.

**How was Mount Kalaga confirmed?**
Rockstar Games officially confirmed Mount Kalaga through 'Visit Leonida' postcards posted on their website in May.

**Is the presence of mountains considered immersion-breaking?**
This is a point of debate within the GTA 6 community, with some arguing it breaks realism and others viewing it as a creative design choice for gameplay purposes.
