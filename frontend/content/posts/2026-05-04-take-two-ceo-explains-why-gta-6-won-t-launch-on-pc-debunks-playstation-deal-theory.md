# Take-Two CEO Explains Why GTA 6 Won't Launch on PC, Debunks PlayStation Deal Theory

Grand Theft Auto VI is set to launch on November 19 for PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X|S, but PC players will have to wait for its release. Take-Two CEO Strauss Zelnick clarified that the decision for a console-first launch isn't due to any co-marketing deal with PlayStation, but rather a long-standing Rockstar Games tradition of prioritizing console editions. Zelnick stated that this strategy focuses on 'serving the core consumer' first and best, and could also create a valuable 'second selling cycle' for the game when it eventually arrives on PC. While acknowledging the significant growth of the PC market, which now accounts for a large percentage of sales for major titles, Zelnick reiterated Rockstar's historical approach. A former Rockstar developer, Mike York, also suggested the move prioritizes 'what sells.' Zelnick expressed both excitement and terror regarding GTA 6's release, citing immense expectations for the title, which is projected to sell 40 million copies and generate over $3 billion in revenue in its first year. The game's price point remains unannounced, though Zelnick promised a 'reasonable' level.

## FAQ
**When is GTA 6 launching?**
Grand Theft Auto VI is scheduled to launch on November 19 for PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X|S.

**Will GTA 6 be available on PC at launch?**
No, GTA 6 will not be available on PC at launch, following Rockstar Games' historical console-first release strategy.

**Why isn't GTA 6 coming to PC at launch?**
According to Take-Two CEO Strauss Zelnick, Rockstar prioritizes 'serving the core consumer' (console players) first. He also noted it creates a potential 'second selling cycle' for the game later on PC.

**Is a PlayStation deal preventing GTA 6 from launching on PC?**
No, Take-Two CEO Strauss Zelnick explicitly denied that a Sony co-marketing deal is the reason for the delayed PC launch, stating Rockstar historically releases on console first.

**What are the sales expectations for GTA 6?**
GTA 6 is expected to sell 40 million copies and generate over $3 billion in revenue within its first year.

**What will be the price of GTA 6?**
Rockstar has not announced an official price, but Take-Two CEO Strauss Zelnick stated it would be 'reasonable.' Bank of America previously suggested a price point of $80.
