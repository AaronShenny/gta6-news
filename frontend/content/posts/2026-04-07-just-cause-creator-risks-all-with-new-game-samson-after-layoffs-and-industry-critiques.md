# Just Cause Creator Risks All with New Game 'Samson' After Layoffs and Industry Critiques

Christofer Sundberg, the outspoken co-creator of the Just Cause series, is launching his new studio Liquid Swords' debut title, 'Samson: A Tyndalston Story,' on April 8 for PC. This gritty crime game comes after a challenging development period, including significant layoffs that forced a re-scoping from a planned AAA experience to a more focused AA title with around 25 hours of gameplay. Sundberg, who founded Liquid Swords on a 'zero-nonsense' philosophy to combat what he calls the 'distractions' and 'crap' of modern game development, emphasizes that Samson's success is 'make-or-break' for the studio. He candidly discusses the financial and emotional investment, the decision to lay off half the team to ensure survival, and his broader criticisms of the industry's current 'quagmire' of mass layoffs, lack of innovation, and over-reliance on remakes. Despite the immense pressure and acknowledged launch imperfections, Sundberg remains passionate and optimistic about the team's creative, focused effort.

## Key Takeaways
- Liquid Swords, founded by Just Cause co-creator Christofer Sundberg, is releasing its debut game, 'Samson: A Tyndalston Story,' on April 8 for PC.
- The studio faced significant challenges, including laying off half its development staff, leading to a reduced scope for Samson from AAA to a more focused AA experience (approx. 25 hours).
- Samson is a gritty crime game where players embody Samson, paying off mobsters after a failed heist, with a debt system, payment quotas, and deadlines.
- Sundberg views modern game development as plagued by 'distractions' and 'crap,' advocating for a 'zero-nonsense' approach, and is financially and emotionally invested in Samson's success.
- He criticizes the broader game industry for its 'quagmire' of mass layoffs, lack of strategy, courage, and over-reliance on remakes, urging for innovation and brutal honesty.
- Despite acknowledging potential launch bugs and the high stakes, Sundberg and his team remain passionate, creative, and committed to their vision.
- The game will eventually aim for a console release, and a $30 Supporter Edition is available.

## FAQ
**Who developed Samson: A Tyndalston Story?**
Samson was developed and is being self-published by Liquid Swords, a studio founded by Christofer Sundberg, co-creator of the Just Cause series.

**When does Samson: A Tyndalston Story release?**
The game releases on April 8 for PC via Steam and the Epic Games Store, with console releases planned for 'eventually.'

**What kind of game is Samson: A Tyndalston Story?**
It's a gritty, focused AA-level crime action game with a debt system where players must pay off mobsters after a botched heist.

**Why was Samson's scope reduced?**
Due to significant layoffs and budget constraints, Liquid Swords had to scale back the game from its original AAA ambitions to a more focused AA experience, cutting features like heavy-RPG systems and base-building.

**What is Christofer Sundberg's view on the current game industry?**
Sundberg is highly critical, describing the industry as a 'quagmire' with 'crap' decisions, a lack of strategy and courage, and an over-reliance on remakes, advocating for more innovation and honesty.

**What are the stakes for Liquid Swords and Samson?**
The launch of Samson is described as 'make-or-break' for Liquid Swords, with Sundberg personally and financially invested in its success after making tough decisions to keep the studio afloat.
