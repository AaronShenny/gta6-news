# 6 months left recap.

This article, titled "6 months left recap," offers a reflective look at the Grand Theft Auto VI community's emotional rollercoaster leading up to the game's final release. It chronicles the prolonged wait since Rockstar's last major titles, the initial announcement four years prior, and the significant September 2022 leaks by Arion Kurtaj. Following an extensive period of silence from Rockstar, the highly anticipated Trailer 1 dropped in December 2023, becoming a global phenomenon and announcing a "2025" release. The community then spent over 500 days meticulously dissecting every frame, leading to a period of growing frustration over the lack of new information. A major content drop occurred in May 2025, revealing Trailer 2, numerous screenshots, and new web content, which unfortunately also brought the first delay to May 2026. A second, 'final' delay pushed the release date to November 19th, 2026. The article concludes with the community currently experiencing cycles of 'Hopium/Copium,' battling fake leaks, and desperately awaiting Trailer 3, just six months before the confirmed launch.

## Key Takeaways
- Grand Theft Auto VI was officially announced four years prior to the article's timeline.
- Major leaks, including over 90 videos, occurred on September 18th, 2022, perpetrated by Arion Kurtaj.
- Rockstar's first official Trailer 1 was released on December 4th, 2023, setting a record for views and announcing a '2025' release.
- A significant info dump, including Trailer 2, 70 screenshots, and a new webpage, was shadow-dropped on May 6th, 2025.
- The game experienced two delays: first to May 26th, 2026, and then to its 'final' release date of November 19th, 2026.
- As of the article's writing, the community is six months away from the final release date, eagerly anticipating Trailer 3 and more information.
