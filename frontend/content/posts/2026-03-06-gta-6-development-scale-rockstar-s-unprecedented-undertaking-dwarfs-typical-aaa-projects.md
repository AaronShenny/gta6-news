# GTA 6 Development Scale: Rockstar's Unprecedented Undertaking Dwarfs Typical AAA Projects

A recent analysis highlights the colossal scale of Grand Theft Auto VI's development compared to standard AAA games. While a typical AAA title involves 100-300 core developers over 3-5 years, GTA 6 is a different beast entirely. Rockstar Games, leveraging its 5,000-6,000 employees across multiple studios, has reportedly been working on the title for 7-8 years. When measured in "person-years"—the equivalent of one developer working for one year—GTA 6 is estimated to exceed 15,000 person-years. This figure drastically overshadows the 1,000-2,000 person-years for a typical AAA game and even the 6,000-7,000 estimated for Rockstar's own Red Dead Redemption 2, underscoring the game's immense scope and ambition.

## Key Takeaways
- Typical AAA games involve 100-300 core developers over 3-5 years.
- Rockstar Games employs 5,000-6,000 staff, many contributing to GTA 6.
- GTA 6's development is estimated to have spanned 7-8 years.
- In "person-years" (one developer for one year):
- Typical AAA game: 1,000-2,000 person-years.
- Huge open-world games: 5,000+ person-years.
- Red Dead Redemption 2: 6,000-7,000 person-years.
- GTA 6 estimate: 15,000+ person-years.
- The scale of GTA 6's development is unprecedented, equating to decades of work at a smaller AAA studio.
