# Unfair Comparisons? Why Early GTA 6 Screenshots Are Being Misjudged Against Linear AAA Titles

A recent discussion highlights the premature and often unfair comparisons being drawn between early, zoomed-in screenshots of *Grand Theft Auto 6* and highly detailed linear AAA titles such as *The Last of Us*. The core argument posits that these comparisons are flawed because they fail to account for the vastly different technical requirements and design philosophies of open-world games versus linear experiences. *Grand Theft Auto 6*, as an expansive open-world title, must simultaneously render an immense map, dynamic traffic, numerous NPCs, complex physics, and intricate weather systems. This stands in stark contrast to linear games, which are designed around smaller, more controlled environments, allowing for a much higher level of detail in specific close-ups, like facial renders. The author emphasizes the absurdity of judging a game's overall visual fidelity and quality based on a few compressed pixels from early trailer footage, especially when the game is still unreleased. The sentiment is that current expectations are potentially too high, leading to unwarranted criticism and a skewed perception of the game's potential before players have even experienced a full playthrough.

## Key Takeaways
- People are unfairly comparing zoomed-in, compressed GTA 6 screenshots to linear AAA games.
- Linear games benefit from smaller, controlled environments, allowing for higher detail in specific areas.
- GTA 6, as an open-world title, must simultaneously render a massive map, traffic, NPCs, physics, and weather systems.
- Judging GTA 6's graphics based on limited, early trailer screenshots is premature and unfair.
- Current expectations for GTA 6's visual fidelity might be excessively high.
