# Fan Analysis Suggests GTA 6 Development Could Date Back to 2016

A recent Reddit analysis by user /u/zathermos has delved into the version numbering system observed in various Grand Theft Auto 6 leaks, proposing an intriguing timeline that places the game's development start as early as late 2016. By linearly plotting version numbers from the extensive 2022 'teapot' leaks (which spanned v400.0 to v542.0 between March 2021 and September 2022), the analyst estimated key development milestones. This methodology suggests that v1.0 of GTA 6 occurred in late 2016—a period that notably aligns with Rockstar's known scouting for locations in Florida and the preliminary stages of 'Project Americas' while Red Dead Redemption 2 was nearing completion. The analysis also accurately places other known leaks, such as a PDF screenshot to September 2019 (v270.0) and the December 2023 TikTok leak to v636.0, lending credibility to the linear progression theory. While the author acknowledges the assumption of a consistently linear versioning system, the findings offer a fascinating glimpse into the game's potentially extensive development cycle.
