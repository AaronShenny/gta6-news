# GTA 6: Community Calls for Robust Anti-Modding Security to Protect Players and Streamers

A concerned Grand Theft Auto community member, who also streams, has voiced significant frustrations regarding the disruptive and often malicious actions of modders in GTA Online. The user, /u/solarcalipsse, detailed experiences where modders would deliberately crash their entire PC, rendering Task Manager inaccessible and forcing a hard reset—a particularly damaging issue for content creators who are frequently targeted. With the extensive development time invested in GTA 6, there's a strong hope within the community that Rockstar Games will prioritize and implement vastly improved security measures, potentially integrating advanced anti-cheat systems like BattleEye, to safeguard the player experience and ensure a more stable online environment.

## Key Takeaways
- Malicious modders in GTA Online are causing severe disruptions, including PC crashes and disabling system controls like Task Manager.
- Streamers are frequently targeted by these disruptive modders, impacting their ability to play and create content.
- The community hopes GTA 6 will feature robust anti-cheat systems, such as BattleEye, to prevent such issues and enhance player security.

## FAQ
**What is the primary concern regarding modders in GTA Online?**
The main concern is that malicious modders can crash players' entire PCs, disable access to Task Manager, and force hard resets, making the game unplayable and highly frustrating.

**Why are streamers particularly affected by malicious modding?**
Streamers are often targeted by modders, leading to frequent game disruptions and PC crashes that severely impact their ability to stream and engage with their audience.

**What solution is hoped for in GTA 6?**
Players are hoping for significantly enhanced anti-cheat security measures in GTA 6, potentially incorporating advanced systems like BattleEye, to combat malicious modding effectively.
