# Take-Two CEO Strauss Zelnick Dismisses AI's Role in GTA, Confident in GTA 6's Enduring Appeal to Gamers of All Ages

In a recent interview, Take-Two CEO Strauss Zelnick addressed several key topics relevant to the gaming industry, including AI, Roblox, global expansion, and the demographics of gamers. Most notably, Zelnick dismissed the notion that artificial intelligence could develop a game like Grand Theft Auto, labeling it 'laughable.' Pertaining to the highly anticipated GTA 6, he directly challenged concerns about older fans potentially 'aging out' of the franchise. Zelnick firmly asserted that gamers do not abandon hobbies they love, highlighting that the audience for the game is, in fact, continuing to expand. He stated, 'If you have a console and you're over 17, just explain to me how is it that you've decided, 'No, no, GTA 6. Not interested.' I just don't see it.' The interview, which also touched upon the DRAM crisis and Asha Sharma, provided a rare official mention of GTA 6, with community speculation pointing to Take-Two's mid-May investor call for potential further updates.
