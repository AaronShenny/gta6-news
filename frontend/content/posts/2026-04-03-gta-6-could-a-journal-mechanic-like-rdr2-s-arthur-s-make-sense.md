# GTA 6: Could a Journal Mechanic Like RDR2's Arthur's Make Sense?

A recent Reddit discussion on the r/GTA6 subreddit sparked debate over the potential inclusion of a journal mechanic in Grand Theft Auto 6, similar to the one featured in Red Dead Redemption 2. The original poster praised RDR2's journal as an effective way to remind players of past game events and interactions, especially for those who don't complete the game in a single playthrough. However, they also acknowledged that some players might find such a mechanic 'out of date' for a modern title like GTA 6, questioning its overall utility and suggesting a modern twist, such as integrating it as an app on the in-game iFruit phone.

## Key Takeaways
- Discussion centers on implementing a journal mechanic in GTA 6, similar to Arthur Morgan's in RDR2.
- Proponents suggest it helps players recall past game events and interactions.
- Concerns are raised about the mechanic potentially feeling 'out of date' for a modern GTA title.
- A suggestion was made to integrate the journal as an iFruit phone app for a modern feel.
- The discussion also questions the actual usage rate of Arthur's journal by RDR2 players.

## FAQ
**What gameplay mechanic is being discussed for GTA 6?**
The discussion revolves around the potential inclusion of a journal system, similar to Arthur Morgan's in Red Dead Redemption 2.

**What is a perceived benefit of a journal mechanic?**
It can serve as a useful reminder for players about past game events, interactions, and character developments, particularly for those who play intermittently.

**What is a potential drawback or concern about a journal in GTA 6?**
Some players might find a traditional journal 'out of date' or not fitting with the modern, urban setting of Grand Theft Auto 6.

**How could a journal mechanic be modernized for GTA 6?**
One suggestion is to integrate it as an application on the in-game iFruit phone, aligning it with the technological themes of GTA.
