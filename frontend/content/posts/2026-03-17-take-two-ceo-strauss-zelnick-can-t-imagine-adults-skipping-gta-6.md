# Take-Two CEO Strauss Zelnick "Can't Imagine" Adults Skipping GTA 6

Take-Two CEO Strauss Zelnick has expressed absolute faith in Grand Theft Auto 6, stating he cannot fathom any reason a grown adult with a console would opt out of purchasing Rockstar's upcoming title. Zelnick believes the game will effortlessly engage new players, dismissing concerns they might be deterred by a lack of experience with prior GTA installments. He also strongly refutes the idea of adults aging out of gaming, comparing it to enduring music preferences formed in one's youth, and asserts that the gaming cohort continues to grow. While confident in the game's market penetration, Zelnick acknowledges that success ultimately hinges on Rockstar delivering a 'great' product. He also touched upon AI's role, stating it's used for efficiency but could never create a game like GTA 6. The game is currently scheduled for release on November 19, 2026.

## Key Takeaways
- Take-Two CEO Strauss Zelnick is supremely confident in GTA 6's universal appeal to adult console owners.
- Zelnick believes new players will easily engage with GTA 6, regardless of their experience with previous titles.
- He dismisses the notion of adults 'aging out' of video games, suggesting entertainment preferences established at age 17 persist.

- AI is viewed as an efficiency tool, not a creative replacement for human developers in complex titles like GTA 6.
- GTA 6 is currently scheduled for release on November 19, 2026.
