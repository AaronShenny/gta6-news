# Rockstar Games Hit By Another Cyberattack Ahead of GTA 6 Launch

Rockstar Games, developer of the highly anticipated Grand Theft Auto 6, has once again been targeted by a cyberattack. The ransomware group ShinyHunters claims to have breached servers belonging to Anodot, a third-party cloud provider used by Rockstar, and threatens to publish stolen data after their ransom demands were not met. Rockstar confirmed the incident to Kotaku, stating that only a "limited amount of non-material company information" was accessed and that they have no intention of paying the ransom. The news briefly impacted parent company Take-Two Interactive's share price, causing a temporary dip of over 6%. This latest security incident follows a history of breaches for Rockstar, including a significant GTA 6 gameplay leak in 2022 by a UK teenager, and recent controversial layoffs of over 30 staffers, which some claim were retaliatory for unionization efforts. Despite these challenges, GTA 6 remains on track for its November 19 release on PS5 and Xbox Series X|S, though reports suggest its online mode may not be available at launch.

## Key Takeaways
- Rockstar Games experienced another cyberattack, attributed to the ransomware group ShinyHunters.
- The breach occurred via a third-party cloud provider, Anodot, with ShinyHunters threatening to release data due to unmet demands.
- Rockstar confirmed the hack, stating only "limited, non-material company information" was accessed, and they will not pay the ransom.
- Take-Two Interactive's stock saw a brief dip of over 6% following the news, later rebounding.
- This incident adds to Rockstar's recent security challenges, including a major GTA 6 gameplay leak in 2022 and controversial staff layoffs.
- Grand Theft Auto 6 is scheduled for release on November 19 for PS5 and Xbox Series X|S, with its online mode potentially delayed.
