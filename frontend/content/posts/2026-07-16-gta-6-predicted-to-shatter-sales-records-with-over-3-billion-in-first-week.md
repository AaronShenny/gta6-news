# GTA 6 Predicted to Shatter Sales Records with Over $3 Billion in First Week

Gaming research firm Newzoo has released staggering sales predictions for Rockstar's highly anticipated Grand Theft Auto 6, which is set to launch in November. The firm estimates that GTA 6 could generate an unprecedented **$3.3 billion to $5.2 billion** in global sales within its first week alone. Even at the lower end, this would significantly outperform GTA 5, which earned $1 billion in its first 72 hours back in 2013.

Newzoo's bullish outlook is based on its proprietary Game Performance Monitor and strong preorder indicators. The firm estimates digital spending on GTA 6 preorders in the US and five major European markets reached $180 million in the final week of June. Extrapolating this globally, based on GTA 5's player distribution, suggests a worldwide preorder figure of approximately $260 million for the first week.

Classifying GTA 6 as a "Proven Sequel"—a massively anticipated entry in an established series with a dedicated audience—Newzoo expects a front-loaded sales curve. They estimate preorders account for about 5.8% of launch week sales, implying a total of **$4.5 billion** in sales and **51 million units sold** at an assumed $88 average price point. Even under a more conservative "Sequel with Performance Uncertainty" scenario, the game is still projected to hit $3.3 billion in sales and 37 million units sold in its first week. Newzoo dismisses unconfirmed reports of $1 billion in preorder revenue as "absurd."

Take-Two Interactive, Rockstar's owner, has yet to release any official preorder data, but an update might come during its August earnings briefing. GTA 6 will launch on November 19 for PS5 and Xbox Series X|S, with standard editions priced at $80 and premium editions at $100. Notably, the game is confirmed to be a digital-exclusive release, with no physical discs available.
