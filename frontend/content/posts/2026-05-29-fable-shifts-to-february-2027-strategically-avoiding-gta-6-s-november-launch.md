# Fable Shifts to February 2027, Strategically Avoiding GTA 6's November Launch

Xbox has confirmed that the highly anticipated RPG, Fable, developed by Playground Games, will now launch in February 2027, a significant shift from its original late 2026 target. This delay, as explained by Xbox chief content officer Matt Booty, aims to provide Fable with its own 'window to shine,' a clear indication of Microsoft's strategy to avoid direct competition with Grand Theft Auto 6, which is slated for a November 19, 2026 release. Booty described GTA 6 as one of the 'biggest entertainment moments' of the year, underscoring the formidable challenge it presents to other major titles. The decision to move Fable ensures it won't be overshadowed by Rockstar's juggernaut, a move echoed by Xbox's social media statement about giving the game the 'dedicated moment it deserves.' This strategic spacing also aligns with Xbox's launch plan for Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 4 in October, aiming to provide breathing room for its tentpole titles.

## Key Takeaways
- Fable has been officially delayed to February 2027 by Xbox.
- The delay is a strategic move to give Fable a clear launch window, avoiding direct competition.
- Grand Theft Auto 6 (GTA 6) is set to launch on November 19, 2026, and is considered a major factor in Fable's delay.
- Xbox chief content officer Matt Booty stated the aim is for Fable to have a 'window of its own' to shine.
- The decision reflects Microsoft's intent to manage its major game releases, including Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 4 (October 22, 2026), to maximize their individual impact.
