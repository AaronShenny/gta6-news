# GTA VI Online's User-Generated Content Model Sparks Concerns Among Players

A recent online discussion highlights significant concerns among the gaming community regarding Rockstar's rumored plans for Grand Theft Auto VI Online to heavily feature user-generated content (UGC). The journalist fears this approach could lead to an influx of low-quality experiences, making it difficult to find valuable content amidst 'brainrot RP farms.' There's also apprehension that a UGC-centric model might result in fewer official content updates from Rockstar and potential negative interactions within role-playing communities. The ideal solution, as proposed, would be a clear separation of UGC/RP servers from the standard online experience, akin to Fortnite's distinct modes.

## Key Takeaways
- Concerns raised over GTA VI Online's rumored heavy reliance on user-generated content (UGC).
- Fear that UGC could lead to a proliferation of low-effort, low-quality experiences, making discovery of good content difficult.
- Speculation that a UGC-focused model might result in less frequent official content updates from Rockstar.
- Negative past experiences with role-playing (RP) server communities and their administration are cited.
- A proposed solution is to keep UGC and RP content separate from the standard online experience, similar to Fortnite's distinct modes.
- Acknowledgement that extensive tools could enable the community to create 'awesome worthwhile stuff'.

## FAQ
**What is the main concern about GTA VI Online's UGC?**
The primary concern is that heavy reliance on user-generated content could lead to a flood of low-quality experiences, making it hard for players to find worthwhile content.

**Why does the author worry about official content updates?**
The author speculates that if players are creating most of the content, Rockstar might reduce the frequency of their own major content updates.

**What solution is proposed for UGC and RP content?**
The suggestion is to keep UGC and role-playing content separate from the main online experience, similar to how Fortnite offers distinct modes for Battle Royale and creative content.

**Are there any positives acknowledged regarding UGC?**
Yes, the author acknowledges that with extensive tools, players could create 'awesome worthwhile stuff'.
