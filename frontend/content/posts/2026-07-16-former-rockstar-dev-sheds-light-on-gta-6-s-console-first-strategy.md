# Former Rockstar Dev Sheds Light on GTA 6's Console-First Strategy

Grand Theft Auto 6 is set to be a massive console launch, but PC gamers will have to wait, a common practice for Rockstar Games. Former Rockstar Games producer John Ricchio elaborated on this console-first approach, stating it's a strategic decision to ensure a better final product. According to Ricchio, beginning development with the limitations of console hardware allows the team to "start with the constraints and then extend," making it easier to optimize and add features later. He emphasized that 'shrinking is a lot harder than extending,' meaning it's more challenging to make a game performant if it was initially built for high-end PCs.

Ricchio clarified that this strategy isn't due to platform bias but is a sound business decision, especially for a game as ambitious as GTA 6, where resource allocation across thousands of employees is critical. Take-Two boss Strauss Zelnick echoed similar sentiments, dismissing theories that Sony's co-marketing deal is behind the PC delay. While acknowledging PC as an increasingly important platform, Zelnick, along with former Rockstar developer Mike York, highlighted the studio's intent to 'prioritize what sells.'

Despite the absence of a day-one PC launch, GTA 6 is projected to achieve monumental sales, with estimates suggesting over 40 million copies sold and more than $3 billion in revenue within its first year. Some reports even forecast $3.3 billion to $5.2 billion in global sales in its initial week. The game is currently slated for a November 19 launch on PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X|S.
