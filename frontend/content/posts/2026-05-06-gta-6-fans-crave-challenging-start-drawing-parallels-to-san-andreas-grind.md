# GTA 6 Fans Crave Challenging Start, Drawing Parallels to San Andreas' Grind

A recent discussion on the r/GTA6 subreddit highlights a common sentiment among fans: a desire for a challenging start in Grand Theft Auto 6's single-player campaign. User /u/grashel articulated hopes that players won't begin with significant wealth, preventing an 'easy' early game. Drawing insights from the initial trailers, the user points to protagonists residing in a renovated house and driving an inexpensive car as indicators that players might need to 'grind' for money. This speculation extends to the expectation of frequent location changes and hotel stays, reinforcing the need for financial accumulation. The sentiment is further supported by a nostalgic comparison to Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas, where players started with a mere $100, and in-game expenses made money accumulation a deliberate challenge.

## Key Takeaways
- A Reddit user hopes GTA 6 single-player won't start with a lot of money, making it 'too easy'.
- Observations from trailers (renovated house, cheap car) suggest a need to 'grind' for money early on.
- Frequent area changes and hotel stays are anticipated to further necessitate earning money.
- The user draws a comparison to GTA San Andreas, where players started with only $100 and money was hard to accumulate.
- There's a desire for a more challenging economic progression similar to older GTA titles.
