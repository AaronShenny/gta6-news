# Samson: A Tyndalston Story Launches, Offering a Focused, Gun-Free Alternative to GTA

The debut title from Liquid Swords, 'Samson: A Tyndalston Story,' created by Just Cause founder Christofer Sundberg, is set to launch on April 8 for PC via Steam and the Epic Games Store. Priced at $25 for the standard edition and $30 for a Supporter Edition, the game is being pitched as an experience akin to Grand Theft Auto but without the guns. Players embody Samson, a character described as having 'learned violence before he learned mercy,' who must pay off mobsters after a failed heist, with his sister held as collateral. Gameplay features a unique debt system with payment quotas and deadlines, brawling, and driving weaponized cars, alongside over 25 skill upgrades. The open-world environment includes multiple districts and NPCs who remember player actions.

Sundberg positions 'Samson' as a 'no-nonsense' game, a shorter, more focused action experience reminiscent of 90-minute action films, contrasting with the sprawling nature of modern AAA titles like GTA. He acknowledges the GTA comparisons but sees 'Samson' as an alternative for players seeking something different. Liquid Swords faced challenges, including laying off half its development team in February 2025 to ensure 'long-term stability,' which led to shelving certain RPG features for potential future updates or sequels.

## FAQ
**What is Samson: A Tyndalston Story?**
It's the debut game from Liquid Swords, founded by Just Cause creator Christofer Sundberg, offering a GTA-like experience focused on brawling and driving weaponized cars, without traditional firearms.

**When does Samson launch and on what platforms?**
It launches on April 8, 2024, on Steam and the Epic Games Store for PC. Console availability has not been announced, but inquiries have been made.

**How much does Samson: A Tyndalston Story cost?**
The standard edition is $25, and a Supporter Edition with digital extras like wallpapers, soundtrack, and concept art is available for $30.

**Does Samson feature guns?**
No, the protagonist Samson does not use guns, but players can utilize weaponized cars to complete missions.

**What unique gameplay mechanics does Samson include?**
It features a debt system where players must pay off mobsters after a heist goes wrong, with payment quotas, deadlines, and growing interest if missions fail. NPCs also remember past player actions.
