# FanArcade Aims to Redefine Gaming Engagement with Culture-Driven, Browser-Based Experiences

The article highlights FanArcade, a company founded by Richie Branson, aiming to revolutionize fan engagement through authentic video game experiences. Branson, a former game designer who worked on titles for Harmonix and Epic Games (including Fortnite's Festival mode), established FanArcade after the viral success of his Kendrick Lamar-inspired game, 'Not Like Us.' This success underscored the power of low-barrier-to-entry games for instant audience connection.

FanArcade's latest project, 'Zoo Fighter,' is a browser-based game inspired by the real-life 'Punch the monkey' story. Players take on the role of Punch, fighting bullies to secure his place in an animal sanctuary, advocating for animal welfare over traditional zoos. The game has achieved significant traction with over 1.1 million unique players and 3 million plays, also offering a direct way for players to support sanctuaries.

Branson emphasizes FanArcade's commitment to creating 'authentic' content by building from within culture, ensuring games resonate naturally with audiences rather than feeling forced. He draws on his past experiences, particularly Harmonix's ability to foster superfans through novel gameplay and Fortnite's expertise in integrating pop culture IP. FanArcade prioritizes no-download, browser-based games to eliminate friction, enable instant engagement, and keep pace with cultural trends.

Looking ahead, Branson expresses ambitions beyond casual browser games, including developing console titles (aspiring to create a 'next Baldur's Gate 3') and crafting meaningful, gamified experiences for sensitive topics like addiction treatment and mental health, drawing from his personal journey. FanArcade's long-term vision is to discover the inherent 'game' in every aspect of culture and translate it into interactive experiences.
