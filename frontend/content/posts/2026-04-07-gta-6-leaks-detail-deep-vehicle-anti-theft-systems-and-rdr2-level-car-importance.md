# GTA 6 Leaks Detail Deep Vehicle Anti-Theft Systems and RDR2-Level Car Importance

Recent leaks surrounding Grand Theft Auto 6 suggest a monumental shift in how players will interact with vehicles, moving far beyond the arcade-style mechanics of GTA V. The upcoming title appears to be drawing significant inspiration from Red Dead Redemption 2, elevating cars to a central role in gameplay. Players will encounter sophisticated anti-theft systems, requiring specialized tools like 'slim jims' for older models and 'Immobilizer Bypasses' for luxury vehicles, with Lucia potentially serving as the dedicated hacker. Vehicle theft itself will be more dynamic, with NPCs actively resisting carjacking attempts. Beyond theft, cars will function as mobile bases, storing heavy weapons due to a limited player inventory, and demanding regular maintenance such as washing, refueling, and repairs. A 'Vehicle Compendium' system will encourage exploration, while a strict distinction between legally owned and stolen cars will impact inventory access and police interactions, making stolen vehicles high-risk assets for fences rather than personal rides. This overhaul points towards a deeply immersive experience where every car has a story and every theft carries consequences.

## Key Takeaways
- Vehicle theft will be significantly more complex, requiring specific tools (slim jim, Immobilizer Bypass via PDA) and hotwiring for different car types.
- Luxury vehicles will feature advanced protections, making them harder to steal and triggering alarms, with reinforced glass and door security.
- Carjacking will involve 'Advanced AI,' allowing NPCs to resist, lock doors, or flee, leading to potential 'Steal Car Fail' scenarios.
- Cars will hold similar importance to horses in RDR2, serving as mobile bases for limited player inventory (especially heavy weapons in trunks/backseats).
- Players will need to maintain their vehicles through washing, refueling at gas stations, and repairs at mechanics.
- A 'Vehicle Compendium' system will track discovered vehicles and liveries, akin to RDR2's animal compendium.
- A strict distinction will exist between legally owned and stolen cars, affecting weapon inventory access and increasing police heat.
- Stolen cars will be 'hot,' making them high-risk assets for selling to 'car fences' rather than keeping for personal use.
- The game will feature an advanced witness and police recognition system, where any vehicle seen being entered can be identified by law enforcement.

## FAQ
**Will stealing cars be as easy as in GTA V?**
No, leaks suggest a complete overhaul. Cars will have anti-theft systems requiring specific tools and hotwiring, making theft much more complex, especially for luxury vehicles. NPCs may also actively resist carjacking attempts.

**How important will my car be in GTA 6?**
Very important. Cars are expected to function similarly to horses in Red Dead Redemption 2, serving as mobile inventory hubs for heavy weapons, requiring regular maintenance like washing and refueling, and contributing to a 'Vehicle Compendium' system.
