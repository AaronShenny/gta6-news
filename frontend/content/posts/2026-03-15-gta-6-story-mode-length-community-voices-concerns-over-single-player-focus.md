# GTA 6 Story Mode Length: Community Voices Concerns Over Single-Player Focus

A recent Reddit post from user /u/Professional-Toe-879 has ignited a conversation within the Grand Theft Auto community regarding the potential length and development focus of GTA 6's story mode. The user, a self-proclaimed fan of replaying GTA campaigns multiple times, voiced apprehension that Rockstar Games might heavily prioritize the online component, given the massive financial success of GTA Online. The core concern revolves around whether dedicated single-player enthusiasts will receive a substantial, replayable narrative experience, or if the studio will predominantly chase the lucrative online market, potentially at the expense of the single-player story.

## Key Takeaways
- Community concern about the potential length of GTA 6's story mode.
- Fear that Rockstar Games might over-prioritize the online experience due to GTA V's success.
- User expresses personal enjoyment of replaying GTA story modes multiple times (5-6 times).
- Assumption that GTA 6 will feature an online component similar to GTA V.
- Hope for a robust and replayable single-player narrative experience in GTA 6.

## FAQ
**What is the main concern regarding GTA 6's story mode?**
The primary concern is the potential length of the story mode and whether Rockstar will dedicate enough resources to it, fearing an overemphasis on online play.

**Why are players worried about the single-player experience?**
Players are concerned that Rockstar's immense success with GTA Online in GTA V might lead them to prioritize the online component in GTA 6, potentially at the expense of a deep and replayable single-player narrative.

**Does the community expect an online component in GTA 6?**
Yes, the discussion assumes GTA 6 will have an online mode similar to GTA V, given its past success and profitability.
