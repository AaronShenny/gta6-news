# GTA 6 Community Hopes for a More Realistic and Rewarding Economy

The Grand Theft Auto community is actively discussing their hopes for a more **grounded and realistic economy** in the upcoming Grand Theft Auto 6. A Reddit user, u/Soggy-Garlic-4011, sparked a conversation expressing a strong desire for GTA 6 to emulate the more balanced economic structure found during the **launch era of GTA Online**.The primary concern raised is the current state of GTA V's in-game economy, which has been criticized for its **hyper-inflated prices**. Examples cited include items like a basic stun gun costing an exorbitant $300,000, which many players feel makes the core gameplay loop excessively tedious and unrewarding. This inflation has reportedly diminished the sense of accomplishment and the 'bragging rights' associated with acquiring expensive items, unlike the earlier days of GTA Online where significant purchases felt more impactful.Players are advocating for a **more proportionate and balanced pricing structure** in GTA 6. The sentiment is that such an economy would make the 'grind' for in-game currency feel far more rewarding, aligning the effort invested with the value obtained. The community hopes Rockstar Games takes this feedback into account to create an economic system that enhances player satisfaction and long-term engagement.

## Key Takeaways
- Players desire a grounded and realistic economy in GTA 6.
- The current GTA V economy is criticized for hyper-inflated prices, making the grind tedious.
- Examples like a $300,000 stun gun highlight disproportionate pricing in GTA V.
- Early GTA Online's balanced economy is cited as a positive model for GTA 6.
- A more balanced economy would make effort feel proportionate and purchases more rewarding and impactful.
