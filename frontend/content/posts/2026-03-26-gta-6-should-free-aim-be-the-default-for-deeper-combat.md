# GTA 6: Should Free-Aim Be the Default for Deeper Combat?

A recent opinion piece suggests that Grand Theft Auto 6 should adopt free-aim as its default aiming mechanic, with a complete overhaul of mission design and combat encounters to support it. The author criticizes Rockstar's past approach, particularly in GTA Online and Red Dead Redemption 2, where combat often feels designed for auto-aim, resulting in repetitive 'horde shooter' scenarios with little skill involved. Drawing inspiration from titles like Naughty Dog's Uncharted series and Metal Gear Solid V, which are praised for their methodical encounters, meaningful enemy placement, and engaging gunplay, the piece argues that a shift in design philosophy could dramatically enhance GTA 6's gameplay experience, moving towards more strategic and satisfying combat.

## Key Takeaways
- Advocates for free-aim as the default aiming mechanic in GTA 6.
- Proposes that GTA 6's combat mechanics and mission structures should be explicitly designed around free-aim.
- Criticizes current Rockstar titles (GTA Online, RDR2) for combat designed around auto-aim, leading to 'mindless horde shooter' gameplay.
- Cites Naughty Dog's Uncharted series and Metal Gear Solid V as examples of excellent third-person combat with methodical encounters and diverse AI.
- Believes that a focus on free-aim design could significantly elevate GTA 6's overall gameplay experience.

## FAQ
**What is the primary argument for free-aim in GTA 6?**
The core argument is that free-aim should be the default aiming mechanic, with all combat and mission design built around it, to foster more skillful and engaging gameplay rather than repetitive, auto-aim-centric encounters.
