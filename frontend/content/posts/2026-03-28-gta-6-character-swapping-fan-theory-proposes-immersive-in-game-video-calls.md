# GTA 6 Character Swapping: Fan Theory Proposes Immersive In-Game Video Calls

A fascinating fan theory circulating within the GTA 6 community offers a unique and highly immersive vision for character transitions, aiming to significantly boost player immersion beyond what was seen in GTA V. The proposal suggests replacing traditional menu-based switching with an in-game calling app, likely a parody of WhatsApp, as hinted by leaks. Players would initiate a video call to the desired character, triggering a dynamic sequence where the camera slowly zooms into the phone screen, transitioning seamlessly to the other character's location and activity. This innovative method not only provides a clever way to mask loading screens by loading the new map area during the camera movement but also promises richer character interactions through context-sensitive dialogue during the call, deepening player understanding of protagonists Jason and Lucia.

## Key Takeaways
- Character switching would utilize an in-game calling app, possibly a parody of WhatsApp.
- Players initiate a video call to the character they wish to control (e.g., Jason calling Lucia).
- The transition involves a smooth camera zoom into the phone screen, moving from the current character to the called character's location.
- Characters are shown engaged in activities during the call, similar to how characters in GTA V were already doing something when switched to.
- Dialogue between characters occurs during the transition, offering insight into their personalities and current situations.
- This transition method could potentially hide loading screens, as the game loads the new area during the camera movement.
- Dialogue could be dynamic, reflecting story progression or recent events (e.g., arguments between characters).
- The system aims to enhance immersion, foster more memorable interactions, and deepen player understanding of the protagonists' personalities.
