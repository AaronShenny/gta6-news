# Game Informer's Executive Editor Brian Shea Addresses GTA 6 Fan 'Baiting' Accusations Over Cover Tease

Game Informer's Executive Editor, Brian Shea, has publicly responded to a Grand Theft Auto 6 fan who accused the publication of 'baiting' its audience concerning a recent cover reveal. The fan's grievance stemmed from a teaser hinting at a 'four-word cover story.' Shea clarified the situation, stating, "I don't know how us teasing a four-word cover story is doing anyone dirty when we had a game with four words on our cover. Be disappointed if you want, but your anger is misdirected." This direct address aims to temper fan expectations and correct the narrative surrounding the perceived misdirection in Game Informer's promotional efforts for the highly anticipated title.

## Key Takeaways
- Brian Shea, Game Informer's Executive Editor, responded to a GTA 6 fan.
- The fan accused Game Informer of 'baiting' them regarding a cover reveal.
- The 'baiting' claim was related to a teaser mentioning a 'four-word cover story'.
- Shea clarified that the actual game cover indeed featured a four-word title.
- He advised the fan that their anger was 'misdirected' and not a result of Game Informer's actions.
