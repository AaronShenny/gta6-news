# Take-Two Boss Reveals He Nixed A Legacy Franchise Sequel Due To Lack of Developer Passion

Take-Two CEO Strauss Zelnick recently disclosed a fascinating anecdote about passing on a sequel to one of the company's 'legacy franchises' because the developers pitching it weren't sufficiently enthusiastic. Speaking at the iicon event, Zelnick recounted sensing a lack of genuine excitement from the producer, leading him to immediately halt the project. While the specific franchise remains a mystery, Zelnick used the story to illustrate Take-Two's philosophy of being highly selective with its game projects, prioritizing developer passion over simply churning out sequels.

Zelnick emphasized that Take-Two is indeed exploring new installments for 'all its franchises,' including titles like L.A. Noire, but only if the teams involved are 'super-juiced about doing it.' He further cautioned against rushing sequels, even in response to fan demand, likening it to 'burning the furniture to keep the house warm.' This sentiment aligns with past statements from Rockstar Games regarding 'bandwidth issues' hindering projects like Bully 2, suggesting a broader company-wide commitment to quality and developer enthusiasm.

## Key Takeaways
- Take-Two CEO Strauss Zelnick canceled a sequel to a legacy franchise due to the developer's lack of passion.
- The specific legacy franchise that was passed on remains undisclosed.
- Take-Two prioritizes developer enthusiasm and a 'super-juiced' team as a key factor for greenlighting new projects or sequels.
- Zelnick confirmed Take-Two is exploring new installments for all its franchises, including L.A. Noire, under the condition of developer passion.
- He warned against rushing sequels, even if fans demand them, to avoid compromising quality or 'burning the furniture'.
- Fan-requested games like Bully 2 have previously faced hurdles such as 'bandwidth issues' and timing concerns.

## FAQ

**What is Take-Two's general philosophy on developing sequels?**
