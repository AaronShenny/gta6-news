# Take-Two CEO Dismisses 'Laughable' AI Game Creation, Confirms GTA 6 Did Not Use Generative AI

Take-Two Interactive CEO Strauss Zelnick has reiterated his stance on artificial intelligence in game development, labeling the notion of AI tools creating high-quality, top-selling games with a simple 'push of a button' as 'laughable.' Zelnick emphasized that while AI can assist in asset creation or storyboarding, it does not guarantee a hit in an already saturated market. He highlighted that entertainment success stems from core creative value, not just efficient production, drawing comparisons to AI-generated music and PowerPoint presentations. Zelnick also clarified that while AI is used internally at Take-Two for cost and time efficiencies, Rockstar Games did not employ generative AI in the development of the highly anticipated Grand Theft Auto 6, which is slated for a November 19, 2026 release on PS5 and Xbox Series X|S.

## Key Takeaways
- Take-Two CEO Strauss Zelnick dismisses the idea of AI creating hit games with a simple button press as 'laughable.'
- He argues that AI tools are beneficial for efficiency (e.g., asset creation, storyboarding) but do not guarantee creative success or a hit title.
- Zelnick compares AI-generated content to music that lacks replay value and PowerPoint presentations that don't add core value.
- He views technologies like Google Genie as a benefit for game development, not a threat, despite market overreactions.
- Rockstar Games did not use generative AI for GTA 6 development.
- Take-Two uses AI internally for cost and time efficiencies across its operations.
- GTA 6 is scheduled to launch on November 19, 2026, for PS5 and Xbox Series X|S.
