# Player Speculates on the Viability of Low-Level Crime in GTA 6

A dedicated fan on Reddit, /u/pgo_, has sparked discussion about the potential integration of low-level criminal activities in Grand Theft Auto 6. While expressing a strong desire for features like breaking into houses, selling stolen vehicles, and dealing drugs, the user ultimately concludes that such mechanics may not resonate within the traditional GTA progression. The core argument rests on the series' inherent drive for players to accumulate vast wealth through major heists and missions, rendering petty crimes quickly obsolete and reducing them to mere 'gimmicks' once significant money is acquired. The player fondly recalls the early, money-scarce stages of GTA V as a highlight and hopes for an expansion on this dynamic in GTA 6, yet acknowledges it's unlikely given the franchise's typical wealth-centric narrative. They even point to games like Skyrim and Kingdom Come: Deliverance for a more fulfilling low-level criminal experience.

## Key Takeaways
- Player expresses desire for low-level crime options in GTA 6 (e.g., house breaking, selling stolen cars/drugs).
- Concludes that these activities might not work due to GTA's story mode progression towards immense wealth.
- Argues that major missions quickly make petty crimes irrelevant and 'gimmicky'.
- Highlights enjoyment of early GTA V where money scarcity made small gains meaningful.
- Believes GTA 6 will likely follow a similar high-wealth progression, diminishing the impact of low-level crime.
- Suggests other games like Skyrim and Kingdom Come: Deliverance offer better low-level criminal gameplay.

## FAQ
**Will GTA 6 feature low-level crime options?**
While a player expresses a strong desire for such options, they speculate that these might not be meaningfully integrated into the game's long-term progression due to the rapid accumulation of wealth from major story missions.

**Why might low-level crime not work in GTA 6 according to the player?**
The player argues that the core narrative of GTA games pushes players towards significant financial gains through heists and major missions, making the small payouts from petty crimes quickly insignificant and reducing them to mere novelties.

**What aspects of previous GTA games does the player appreciate?**
The player particularly enjoyed the early stages of GTA V, where characters were broke and money was scarce, making every financial gain, no matter how small, feel impactful and meaningful.
