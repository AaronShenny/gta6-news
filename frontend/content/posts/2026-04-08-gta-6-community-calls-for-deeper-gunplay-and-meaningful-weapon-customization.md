# GTA 6: Community Calls for Deeper Gunplay and Meaningful Weapon Customization

A dedicated Grand Theft Auto fan has taken to Reddit to express their fervent hopes for an extensive overhaul of both gunplay and weapon customization in the upcoming Grand Theft Auto VI. Citing Grand Theft Auto V as 'underwhelming' in these areas, the user detailed frustrations with generic weapon feel, a lack of recoil or weight, and a shallow customization system where attachments offered minimal gameplay impact and were simply bought with in-game currency. For GTA VI, the community member envisions a world where each weapon feels distinct through unique sound, recoil, and handling, offering actual strategic reasons to choose one firearm over another. They also advocate for a deeper customization system, allowing for impactful builds through specific parts like stocks, barrels, and grips, rather than just superficial stat boosts. Crucially, the post suggests a progression system where weapons and attachments are earned through completing missions, specific objectives, or discovering rare items and contacts, rather than simply grinding for cash. This shift, they argue, would imbue gear with far more meaning and engagement. The post concludes by posing the question of whether Rockstar will truly innovate or merely offer a slightly improved version of GTA V's mechanics.

## Key Takeaways
- GTA V's gunplay and weapon customization were criticized for being 'underwhelming' and 'shallow'.
- Specific criticisms included generic weapon feel, lack of impactful recoil/weight, and superficial customization that barely affected gameplay.
- Hopes for GTA VI gunplay include unique weapon identity (sound, recoil, handling) and strategic reasons to choose specific firearms.
- Proposed customization improvements involve a deeper system with specific parts (stocks, barrels, grips) that create gameplay-altering builds, moving beyond simple stat boosts.
- The community advocates for a progression system where weapons and attachments are unlocked through missions, objectives, and contacts, rather than solely through monetary purchases.
- The ultimate goal is to make weapons feel more 'meaningful' by earning them through gameplay experiences.

## FAQ
**What were the main criticisms of GTA V's gunplay?**
The gunplay in GTA V was criticized for making most weapons feel similar, lacking significant weight or recoil, and not offering compelling reasons to switch firearms, leading to a shallow experience.

**How was weapon customization in GTA V perceived?**
GTA V's weapon customization was seen as basic, with the same attachments available for almost everything. These attachments barely affected gameplay and the system was shallow, often reduced to simply buying everything available.

**What improvements are hoped for in GTA VI's gunplay?**
Players hope for unique weapon identities with distinct sounds, recoil, and handling. The desire is for more weight and impact when shooting, providing actual reasons to choose one gun over another based on tactical needs.

**What kind of weapon customization is desired for GTA VI?**
The community wants a deeper customization system that includes specific parts like stocks, barrels, and grips. The goal is for these customizations to create builds that genuinely change gameplay, rather than just offering minor stat boosts.

**How do players suggest unlocking gear in GTA VI?**
Instead of merely buying gear with in-game money, players propose unlocking weapons and attachments by completing missions, achieving specific objectives, or finding rare items and contacts, making the acquisition feel more meaningful.
