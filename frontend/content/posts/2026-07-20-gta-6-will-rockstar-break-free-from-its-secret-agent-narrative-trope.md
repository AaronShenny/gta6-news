# GTA 6: Will Rockstar Break Free from Its 'Secret Agent' Narrative Trope?

A dedicated fan on Reddit has sparked a discussion about a recurring narrative pattern observed across several mainline Rockstar Games titles: the protagonist frequently ends up working for secret government agents. The user, /u/R0ZPIERDALAT0R, points out instances in *Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas* (Mike Toreno), *Grand Theft Auto IV* (U.L. Paper), *Grand Theft Auto V* (Steve Haines of the FIB), and *Red Dead Redemption* (Edgar Ross). Even a rumored abandoned DLC for *GTA V* was said to involve Trevor as a secret agent. While acknowledging the 'cool' nature of the trope, the fan argues it has become overused. They express optimism that *Red Dead Redemption 2* successfully moved away from this formula and hope that *Grand Theft Auto 6* will follow suit, offering a fresh narrative direction. Despite this specific desire, the user candidly admits they will 'devour this game regardless.'

## Key Takeaways
- Rockstar's mainline titles often feature protagonists working for secret agents.
- Examples include GTA San Andreas, GTA IV, GTA V, and Red Dead Redemption.
- The trope is considered cool but overused by the fan.
- The fan hopes GTA 6 will avoid this narrative pattern, similar to Red Dead Redemption 2.
- Despite narrative preferences, the fan will still eagerly play GTA 6.
