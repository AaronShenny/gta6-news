# GTA 6 Customization: Will Vice City Offer Specialized Mod Shops or a Central Hub?

Recent discussions among the Grand Theft Auto community are buzzing about the potential evolution of vehicle customization in GTA 6. Drawing insights from the game's trailers and its anticipated Miami/Florida-inspired setting, players are speculating whether Rockstar Games will move away from the centralized 'Los Santos Customs' model seen in GTA V. The prevailing theory suggests a more segmented approach, with multiple, specialized workshops focusing on specific car parts—such as dedicated shops for wheels, engine tuning, exterior modifications, and potentially even interior customization. This shift, if implemented, would aim to enhance the tuning culture within the game, reflecting real-world trends in custom car modification prevalent in the region, while maintaining gameplay fluidity amidst the project's massive scale.

## Key Takeaways
- GTA 6 trailers hint at a strong emphasis on car tuning and heavily customized vehicles.
- The Miami/Florida setting often features specialized custom car shops, particularly for specific parts like wheels.
- Speculation suggests a move from GTA V's single, centralized customization shop to multiple specialized ones in GTA 6.
- Potential specialized shops could include those for wheels, engine mechanics, exterior visuals, and interior customization.
- This segmented approach is seen as fitting the 'massive scale' of GTA 6 and offering a more detailed customization experience.
